📋 High-Yield Overview
🔍
⚠️ Top 3 Exam Priorities:
1. Portal vein forms posterior to the neck (union of SMV + splenic vein)
2. Left suprarenal vein → left renal vein (must ligate before left nephrectomy)
3. Ventral bud → uncinate + lower head | Dorsal bud → body + tail + rest of head
🥞 Pancreas — Structure
📍
Location: Retroperitoneal, upper posterior abdominal wall. Soft, lobulated, elongated mixed gland.
🫀
Head: In concavity of duodenum. Posterior: IVC, CBD, aorta.
🔗
Neck: Posterior: Portal vein (SMV + splenic). Anterior: pyloroduodenal junction.
📐
Body: Triangular cross-section. Anterior surface separated from stomach by lesser sac.
🔚
Tail: Passes through lienorenal ligament → reaches hilum of spleen.
🩺 Pancreas — Clinical Pearls
Cancer Head of Pancreas causes:
• CBD compression → obstructive jaundice
• IVC compression → lower limb edema
• Portal vein → portal hypertension
⚠️
Pancreas looks like fat on surgery — detected late. Proximity to aorta, IVC, portal vein = rapid spread.
🔬
Peptic ulcer posterior wall → can erode anterior surface of pancreas (separated only by lesser sac) → acute pancreatitis.
🩸 Blood Supply
🔴
Arterial: Superior pancreaticoduodenal a. + Inferior pancreaticoduodenal a. + Splenic artery branches
🔵
Venous: Portal v., Superior mesenteric v., Splenic v.
⚡
Innervation: Sympathetic (thoracic splanchnic) = inhibitory. Parasympathetic (vagus) = stimulatory (both exocrine + endocrine).
🎯
Ampulla of Vater opens at major duodenal papilla on posteromedial wall of 2nd part duodenum.

🔬 Suprarenal Embryology
🩸
Cortex: From mesoderm → 3 zones (glomerulosa, fasciculata, reticularis)
🧠
Medulla: From neural crest cells (ectoderm) → chromaffin cells
📅
Fetal cortex (2nd trimester): acidophilic cells secrete DHEA → converted to estrogen by placenta
💊
Pheochromocytoma: Chromaffin cell tumor → excess epinephrine/norepinephrine → paroxysmal HTN, tachycardia, headache. 25% familial = RET gene
🔢 Key Numbers
📅
7th week: Ventral bud carried dorsally to fuse with dorsal bud
🧪
3rd month: Islets of Langerhans develop from parenchyma
💉
5th month: Islets begin secreting insulin
👶
Neonatal: Fetal cortex involutes; 3 definitive cortical layers form postnatally
📊
25%: Pheochromocytomas that are familial (RET gene)

⚠️ Congenital Anomalies
🔴 Annular Pancreas
Ventral bud splits: part rotates left in front of duodenum, rest rotates behind → ring of pancreatic tissue → duodenal obstruction. Presents as bilious vomiting in neonate.
🔵 Ectopic Pancreatic Tissue
Found in mucosa of stomach, duodenum, or Meckel's diverticulum. May cause pain, bleeding, or obstruction.
