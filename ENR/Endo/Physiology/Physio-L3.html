🦋 Thyroid Gland — Complete Study Guide

The thyroid is not essential for life, but essential for growth, mental well-being, and metabolic homeostasis. It is the master regulator of basal metabolic rate. Master 4 core topics: synthesis, transport, functions, and regulation/disorders.

📌 Core Concept Cards

🔬 Histology

Structure

Follicular cells surround a colloid-filled cavity of thyroglobulin. Parafollicular (C) cells secrete calcitonin for calcium homeostasis — NOT T3/T4.

⚖️ T3 vs T4 Comparison EXAM

High-Yield

T4 = 90% secretion, less potent, 4× longer duration. T3 = 10% secretion, 4× more potent. T4 converts to T3 in liver/kidney. Both lipophilic → nuclear receptor action.

🧪 Enzyme: Thyroid Peroxidase

Exam-Favorite

Essential for 3 steps: (1) Oxidation of I⁻ → I₂, (2) Iodination of tyrosine (MIT, DIT), (3) Coupling (MIT+DIT=T3; DIT+DIT=T4). Blocked by PTU/methimazole.

🩸 Free = Active EXAM

Clinical Pearl

Only 0.03% T4 and 0.3% T3 are free. >99% bound to TBG (70-80%), albumin, prealbumin. Bound = storage. Free = biologically active. Pregnancy ↑TBG but free stays normal.

💥 BMR Mechanism

Mechanism

T3 binds nuclear receptor → activates DNA → new protein synthesis. Increases number & size of mitochondria → ↑ATP + ↑heat production = ↑BMR and O₂ consumption.

⚡ TSI — No Feedback!

Graves' Disease

TSI mimics TSH, stimulates thyroid continuously. Unlike TSH, it has NO negative feedback from high T3/T4 → unregulated hyperthyroidism = Graves' disease.

👶 Cretinism vs Myxoedema

Disorders

Infant hypothyroid = Cretinism (dwarfism, mental retard, macroglossia, potbelly, carotenaemia). Adult hypothyroid = Myxoedema (cold intolerance, weight gain, dry skin, bradycardia, slow memory).

🥬 Wolff-Chaikoff Effect

Regulation

Excess iodine inhibits thyroid hormone synthesis (organification). Used clinically before thyroid surgery. Gland "escapes" after ~14 days. Also: goitrogens in cabbage (thiocyanates) block iodide pump.

🔄 Synthesis Flowchart
1
Thyroglobulin Synthesis
Follicular cells synthesize thyroglobulin (a large glycoprotein)
↓
2
Iodide Trapping
Active transport (iodine pump) concentrates iodide from plasma into follicular cells — requires energy
↓
3
Oxidation (Peroxidase)
Thyroid peroxidase oxidizes I⁻ → I₂ (active iodine)
↓
4
Iodination of Tyrosine
In the colloid: I₂ added to tyrosines on thyroglobulin → MIT (1 iodine) and DIT (2 iodines)
↓
5
Coupling Reaction
MIT + DIT = T3 | DIT + DIT = T4 (all by thyroid peroxidase)
↓
6
Storage in Colloid
T3 & T4 stored in peptide linkage with thyroglobulin in the follicular cavity
↓
7
TSH → Release
TSH stimulates endocytosis of thyroglobulin → lysosomes + proteases cleave T3/T4 → diffuse into blood
🏥 Systemic Effects Quick Reference

❤️ CVS

↑HR, ↑Stroke Volume, ↑CO, ↓Peripheral Resistance. Potentiates β1-adrenergic catecholamines.

🫁 Respiratory

↑Ventilation rate & depth to meet increased O₂ demands.

🧠 Nervous System

↑Beta-adrenergic responses, CNS hyperexcitability, tremors, insomnia in excess.

🍽️ GIT

↑Appetite, ↑secretions, ↑motility → diarrhea in hyperthyroidism.

🦴 Bone

Promotes ossification and epiphyseal fusion. Essential for normal skeletal growth.

🩸 Haemopoietic

↑Erythropoietin → ↑RBC. ↑2,3-DPG → improved O₂ dissociation to tissues.

🥦 Lipid Metabolism

↑Lipolysis, ↑FFA oxidation. ↓Cholesterol via ↑LDL receptors and ↑fecal excretion.

🍬 Carbohydrate

↑Glucose uptake, ↑glycogenolysis, ↑gluconeogenesis, ↑GIT glucose absorption.

💊 Drugs

↑Clearance of drugs, e.g. digitalis. ↑Vitamin A synthesis from carotenes.

⚠️ Inhibitory Factors

🥬 Goitrogens

Thiocyanates in cabbage/turnips block iodide trapping → ↓T3/T4 → ↑TSH → goiter

🧪 Wolff-Chaikoff

Excessive I⁻ intake → inhibits organification → temporary ↓synthesis (~14 days then escape)

💊 Anti-thyroid Drugs

PTU/methimazole block thyroid peroxidase. Radioactive iodine destroys follicular cells.

📉 ↓TBG

Androgens, glucocorticoids decrease TBG → more free T3/T4 available initially

I⁻ Deficiency

Insufficient substrate → ↓synthesis. ↑TSH → goiter (endemic goiter in iodine-poor regions)

👴 Aging

Aging is listed as an inhibitory factor for thyroid hormone secretion
