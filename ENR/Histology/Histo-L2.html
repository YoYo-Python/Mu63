Menstrual Cycle Phases
Hormonal control → Histological consequence

🔴 Menstrual
Days 1–5

Corpus luteum dies → ↓ Progesterone → Spiral arteries constrict → Ischemia → Functional layer sheds

🔵 Proliferative
Days 6–14

Follicles mature → ↑ Estrogen → Epithelium, stroma & glands proliferate via mitosis → Spiral arteries re-grow

🟢 Secretory
Days 15–28

Corpus luteum secretes → ↑ Progesterone → Glands dilate & tortuous → Glycogen accumulates → Ready for implantation

⚠️ High-Yield Exam Facts
Direct from lecture notes — these WILL be tested
🏆
Myometrium in pregnancy: Smooth muscle cells undergo both hypertrophy AND hyperplasia, AND take on a fibroblast-like role synthesizing collagen to strengthen the uterine wall.
🏆
Placental barrier in late pregnancy: The cytotrophoblast disappears from the 4th–5th month onwards. Late barrier = Syncytiotrophoblast + basement membrane + mesenchyme + capillary basement membrane + endothelium (NO cytotrophoblast).
🏆
Mammary gland type: Compound branched tubuloalveolar gland. 15–25 lobes, each with its own lactiferous duct. This is an explicit exam question per the lecture.
🏆
Vaginal defense: The vagina has NO glands. Glycogen is made by the squamous cells themselves, metabolized by flora into lactic acid — maintaining acidity.
🏆
Basal layer survival: Straight arteries supplying the stratum basalis do NOT respond to progesterone withdrawal — hence the basal layer survives menstruation and regenerates the functional layer.
⚠️ EXAM ALERT: "During pregnancy, smooth muscle cells take a second function: synthesizing collagen to make the uterine wall strong." — Direct lecture quote.
⚠️ EXAM ALERT: "If asked about the placental barrier in late pregnancy, say everything EXCEPT cytotrophoblast." — Direct lecture quote.
⚠️ EXAM ALERT: Endocervix = simple columnar mucus-secreting. Exocervix = non-keratinized stratified squamous. Junction is abrupt at the external os.

Menstrual Phase — Step by Step
🔴 Corpus Luteum Degenerates

Day ~28 — progesterone source disappears

📉 Progesterone drops to zero

The key hormonal trigger

🔀 Spiral Arteries Constrict

Hormone-sensitive; supply the stratum functionalis only

⛔ Ischemia develops

Upper 2/3 of endometrium loses blood supply

💀 Necrosis of Stratum Functionalis

Glands, epithelium, and stroma of upper 2/3 die

🩸 Sloughing + Bleeding

Menstrual flow — lasts 3–5 days

✅ Stratum Basalis Survives

Supplied by straight arteries — NOT progesterone-sensitive. Regenerates the functional layer.

Proliferative Phase — Estrogen Drive
🌱 Follicles Mature

Early in cycle — rising FSH

📈 Estrogen Secreted

From granulosa cells of maturing follicles

🔬 Mitosis Stimulated

Surface epithelium, stroma, uterine glands, and spiral arteries all proliferate

📏 Functional Layer Rebuilt

Thin → thick; glands straight and narrow

Secretory Phase — Progesterone Action
🥚 Ovulation Occurs

Day 14 — LH surge

🔵 Corpus Luteum Forms

From ruptured follicle

📈 Progesterone Rises

Main hormone of the secretory phase

🌀 Glands Become Tortuous

Dilated, corkscrew-shaped, filled with glycogen

🍬 Glycogen Stored

Nourishment ready for potential embryo

🤰 If Fertilized → Implantation

Stroma cells become decidual cells (accumulate glycogen, grow processes)

Chorionic Villi Development
Day 2 post-implantation

🔴 Primary Villi

Inner cytotrophoblast (distinct cell boundaries) + Outer syncytiotrophoblast (no boundaries — continuous belt)

2 layers
Day ~15 / Week 2

🔵 Secondary Villi

Primary villi invaded by mesenchymal connective tissue core — gives structural support

3 layers
End of Month 1

🟢 Tertiary Villi

Capillary loops develop within mesenchymal core — fetal circulation established. Now functional!

4 layers + vessels
⚠️ EXAM TIP: Cytotrophoblast = inner layer with DISTINCT cell boundaries. Syncytiotrophoblast = outer CONTINUOUS layer with NO boundaries. The syncytiotrophoblast borders the maternal blood in the intervillous space.
