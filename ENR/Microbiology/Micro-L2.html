📅 HSV Pathogenesis Timeline

Step 1 — Primary Infection

Virus contacts mucous membrane or abraded skin via saliva (HSV-1) or sexual contact (HSV-2). Local multiplication begins immediately.

Step 2 — Vesicular Lesions

Painful vesicular/ulcerative lesions form. HSV-1 → gingivostomatitis, cold sores. HSV-2 → genital ulcers, cervical lesions.

Step 3 — Retrograde Axonal Flow

Virus travels backwards along sensory nerves from the infection site to nerve ganglia. This is how latency is established.

Step 4 — Latency Established

HSV-1 hides in Trigeminal ganglia. HSV-2 hides in Sacral ganglia. Virus is silent — no symptoms, but DNA persists forever.

Step 5 — Reactivation

Triggered by: stress, common colds, sunlight, hormonal changes. Virus reactivates → recurrent lesions OR asymptomatic shedding. Acyclovir cannot prevent this!

🔺 HPV Oncogenesis Mechanism
1
HPV Infects Cervical Epithelium

Direct sexual contact transmits HPV to the transformation zone of the cervix

2
Persistent Infection Occurs

Most infections clear spontaneously via cellular immunity. High-risk types persist in ~10% of cases

3
Viral DNA Integrates into Host Genome

High-risk HPV (16, 18) integrates into host DNA, disrupting tumor suppressor regulation

4
E6 & E7 Oncoproteins Act

E6 protein degrades p53. E7 protein inactivates Rb. Both block normal cell cycle checkpoints

5
Cervical Carcinoma Develops

Uncontrolled cell division → CIN → invasive squamous cell carcinoma of the cervix

🗺️ Diagnostic Algorithm — HSV Encephalitis
1
Clinical Suspicion

Fever + altered consciousness + focal neurological signs + temporal lobe involvement on imaging

2
Lumbar Puncture (LP)

CSF analysis: lymphocytic pleocytosis, elevated protein, elevated RBCs (hemorrhagic encephalitis)

3
PCR of CSF ⭐ Most Sensitive

Detection of HSV-1 DNA in CSF — gold standard for HSV encephalitis diagnosis

4
Start Acyclovir Immediately

Don't wait for PCR results — IV Acyclovir started empirically due to severity. PCR confirms later.

🗺️ Diagnostic Algorithm — Cervical HPV Screening
1
Pap Smear Screening (Age >30, USA guideline)

Cervical scraping examined microscopically for koilocytes — pathognomonic of HPV infection

2
If Koilocytes Present → HPV DNA PCR

PCR identifies specific HPV type — distinguishes low-risk (6,11) from high-risk (16,18) strains

3
Colposcopy if High-Risk Strain Found

Direct visualization of cervix with biopsy for histological confirmation of CIN grade

4
Treatment / Follow-up Based on CIN Grade

CIN 1: watchful waiting. CIN 2/3: LEEP, cryotherapy, or laser ablation.

🌿 HPV Treatment Options
🥶
Cryotherapy
Freezes warts with liquid nitrogen. Works for most warts BUT less effective for genital warts than other types.
⚡
Electrocauterization
Burns off warts using electrical current. Used for genital condylomata. Also less effective than for other wart types.
🔬
Imiquimod (Aldara)
Immunomodulator that stimulates interferon production. Topical application. Boosts local immune response to clear HPV infection.
✂️
Surgical Excision
Physical removal of lesions. LEEP procedure for cervical CIN. Also less effective for genital warts specifically.
💉
HPV Vaccination (Prevention)
VLP vaccine prevents infection. Females aged 10–25. Does NOT treat existing infection.
⏰
Watchful Waiting
Most HPV infections resolve spontaneously via cellular immunity within 1–2 years. Low-risk types observed first.

⚠️ Critical Treatment Pearls
• Acyclovir works by: Phosphorylated by viral thymidine kinase → inhibits viral DNA polymerase → terminates DNA chain
• Cannot cure HSV latency — no drug can eliminate the virus from ganglia. Lifelong suppressive therapy may reduce frequency of recurrences but not eliminate them
• Imiquimod vs Acyclovir: Imiquimod stimulates interferon (immune-based). Acyclovir targets viral DNA replication directly
• Cryotherapy is LESS effective for genital warts than for other wart types — a direct comparison point from the lecture!
• HPV vaccine target: Females aged 10–25. Given BEFORE sexual exposure for maximum efficacy

🔬 "HPV Lab = PAP not Culture"
Diagnosing HPV — what works and what doesn't

❌ Serology → too many serotypes (>100) to distinguish
🔑 "HPV = Highly Particular Virus — refuses to grow in cell cultures!"
