⚠️ #1 High-Yield Exam Distinction
BPH (driven by estrogen, central/periurethral lobe, NO malignant potential) vs Prostatic Carcinoma (driven by androgen, posterior lobe, osteosclerotic bone mets, elevated PSA + Alk Phos). These are completely separate entities with different hormones, locations, and outcomes.

1 · Congenital Anomalies

🔒 Phimosis ⚠️ EXAM
Cause: Anomalous development OR repeated infections → scarring of preputial ring

Result: Cannot retract foreskin over glans

Complications: Balanitis · Urinary obstruction · SCC of penis
CongenitalPre-malignant
🔗 Hypospadias & Epispadias
Hypospadias: Urethra opens on ventral surface (under)
Epispadias: Urethra opens on dorsal surface (top)

Complications: Urinary obstruction · Sterility

Tip: hypo = below = ventral
Structural defect
⬇️ Cryptorchidism ⚠️ EXAM
Def: Arrest of testicular descent → remains undescended

Causes: ↓ Pituitary gonadotrophins · Organic obstruction · Testicular defect

Complications: Atrophy · Fibrosis · Bilateral = infertility · Pre-malignant → Seminoma

⚠️ Must orchidopexy even if no fertility desire — malignancy risk!

Def: Varicosity of pampiniform plexus ("bag of worms")
Primary: Idiopathic (left > right) | Secondary: Venous thrombosis

2 · Prostate Pathology

🔴 BPH ⚠️ HIGH YIELD
Cause: Absolute/relative ↑ estrogen (aging)
Lobe: Periurethral (lateral & middle lobes)
Micro: Hyperplastic glands + stroma; cysts with corpora amylacea
Outcomes: Prostatism → bladder trabeculations → hydroureter → hydronephrosis → chronic renal failure
⚠️ NO relation to malignancy!
Estrogen-drivenBenign
⚫ Prostatic Carcinoma MALIGNANT
Type: Adenocarcinoma
Cause: Genetics + excess androgen
Lobe: Posterior lobe (palpable on DRE)
Spread: Local → lymphatics → osteosclerotic bone mets
Markers: ↑ PSA · ↑ Acid phosphatase · ↑ Alk Phos (bone mets)
Androgen-drivenOsteosclerotic mets

🔍 Prostate Diagnostic Algorithm

🧪 Key Investigations

Digital Rectal Exam (DRE)
BPH: Smooth, symmetrical, rubbery enlargement
Carcinoma: Hard, irregular, fixed nodule — posterior lobe
First-lineNon-invasive
PSA — Prostate Specific Antigen
Significantly elevated in carcinoma. Mildly elevated in BPH/prostatitis. Used for screening, monitoring recurrence after treatment. Abnormal DRE + ↑PSA → biopsy.
↑ in carcinomaMonitoring
Serum Alkaline Phosphatase
Markedly elevated in osteosclerotic bone metastases from prostatic carcinoma. Osteoblast enzyme released during blastic bone remodeling. Classic with lower back pain.
↑ in bone mets
Histopathology (Biopsy)
BPH: Hyperplastic glands, corpora amylacea
Carcinoma: Irregular glands, prominent nucleoli, no basal cells
Seminoma: Sheets of cells + lymphocyte-rich fibrous septa
Gold standard
Renal Function Tests
Serum creatinine, BUN, GFR. Mandatory in BPH workup to assess for obstructive nephropathy progressing to chronic renal failure. Ultrasound for hydronephrosis.
Testicular Tumor Markers
AFP: Yolk sac tumor
HCG: Choriocarcinoma
Androgens ↑: Leydig cell tumor
Estrogens ↑: Sertoli cell tumor
Pre-op staging

🧫 Histological Pearls

Koilocytosis
Perinuclear cytoplasmic vacuolization with nuclear enlargement. Pathognomonic of HPV infection. Seen in condyloma acuminatum. The "hollow cell" hallmark.
Corpora Amylacea
Laminated eosinophilic concentric bodies in lumens of hyperplastic prostatic glands. Classic of BPH. NOT seen in carcinoma — important negative finding.
Lymphocyte-Rich Fibrous Septa
Pathognomonic of seminoma. Fibrous septa dividing sheets of large, clear-cytoplasm germ cells are densely infiltrated by lymphocytes — host immune response.
Schiller-Duval Bodies
Glomeruloid structures with a central vessel within a cystic space. Pathognomonic of yolk sac tumor (endodermal sinus tumor). Always associated with elevated AFP.
Management
Condition-based treatment · Priority tiers
🚨 Urgent / Acute Management
Acute urinary retention (BPH)Emergency catheterization; then α-blockers (tamsulosin)
Prostatic Ca. + bone painAndrogen deprivation (GnRH agonists); bisphosphonates; targeted radiotherapy to bone mets
Choriocarcinoma (aggressive)BEP chemotherapy (Bleomycin + Etoposide + Cisplatin); highly chemosensitive
Testicular torsion vs tumorEmergency scrotal exploration; if tumor → radical orchiectomy + staging
💊 Standard Treatment
BPH (symptomatic)α-blockers (tamsulosin) · 5α-reductase inhibitors (finasteride) · TURP if refractory
Prostatic Ca. (localized)Radical prostatectomy OR radiotherapy (EBRT / brachytherapy)
Prostatic Ca. (advanced)Androgen deprivation (surgical orchidectomy or LHRH agonists) · Docetaxel
SeminomaRadical orchiectomy + radiotherapy (most radiosensitive solid tumor!) · BEP for advanced stages
Non-seminoma GCTsRadical orchiectomy + BEP chemotherapy ± RPLND
CryptorchidismOrchidopexy before age 2 — preserves fertility AND reduces malignancy risk
PhimosisTopical corticosteroids; circumcision if persistent/complicated
Condyloma AcuminatumTopical podophyllin / imiquimod · Cryotherapy · Laser ablation
Varicocele + infertilityVaricocelectomy (surgical or embolization); improves sperm parameters in ~60–70%
📊 Monitoring & Prevention
Post-prostatectomySerial PSA every 3–6 months; rising PSA = recurrence
Post-seminoma treatmentCT surveillance + tumor markers (AFP, HCG) 3-monthly
HPV preventionHPV vaccine (quadrivalent) · Safe sex · Circumcision ↓ risk of penile SCC
⚠️ Exam Key: Seminoma vs Non-Seminoma Treatment
Seminoma = most radiosensitive solid tumor → radiotherapy is definitive even for metastatic disease. Non-seminomas = relatively radioresistant → require BEP chemotherapy. This distinction is essential to state in any exam answer about testicular tumors.
