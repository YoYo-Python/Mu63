Disease Map 4 Major Categories
⬆️ Hyperthyroidism

Graves' Disease

Autoimmune. TSI antibodies activate TSH receptors → unregulated diffuse hyperplasia → excess T3/T4. Triad: Goiter + Exophthalmos + Pretibial myxedema.

⚠️ EXAM: Diffuse & symmetrical enlargement
⬇️ Hypothyroidism

Hashimoto Thyroiditis

CD4+ helper T cells recruit CD8+ cytotoxic T cells + B cells → anti-thyroglobulin & anti-TPO antibodies → follicular destruction → primary hypothyroidism.

⚠️ EXAM: 2nd most common cause of hypothyroidism
🦠 Thyroiditis

De Quervain Thyroiditis

Post-viral. Acute follicular destruction → granulomas with giant cells around extravasated colloid. Self-limiting. Tender gland. Preceding URI.

🪨 Thyroiditis

Riedel Thyroiditis

Idiopathic fibrosclerosis → replaces parenchyma with dense fibrosis → stony hard gland mimicking carcinoma. Can compress trachea & esophagus.

🔵 Goiter

Simple (Non-toxic) Goiter

Iodine deficiency → low T3/T4 → excess TSH → diffuse follicular hyperplasia. Maintains euthyroid state. "Toxic" = hyperfunction, NOT poisoning.

⚠️ EXAM: Iodine deficiency = #1 cause of hypothyroidism globally
🔵 Goiter

Multinodular Goiter

Cyclic hyperplasia → involution → fibrosis, calcification, hemorrhage, cyst formation → asymmetrical nodular surface. Can become toxic (MNG).

🔴 Neoplasm

Follicular Adenoma vs. Carcinoma

Both encapsulated & look identical. ONLY diagnosis = capsular +/− vascular invasion in carcinoma. RAS mutation. Hematogenous spread to lung & bone.

⚠️ EXAM: Invasion = Malignant. No invasion = Benign.
🔴 Neoplasm

Papillary Carcinoma

Most common thyroid cancer. RET mutation or radiation. Nuclear features: grooving + optically clear "Orphan Annie" nuclei + Psammoma bodies. Lymphatic spread → cervical LN. Best prognosis.

🔴 Neoplasm

Follicular Carcinoma

RAS mutation. Hematogenous spread to lungs and bone. Encapsulated but with capsular/vascular invasion. Intermediate prognosis.

🔴 Neoplasm

Medullary Carcinoma

Parafollicular C-cells. RET oncogene point mutation. Secretes calcitonin → amyloid stroma. Associated with MEN syndromes. Both lymphatic & hematogenous spread.

🔴 Neoplasm

Anaplastic Carcinoma

Undifferentiated. P53 mutation. Elderly patients. Extremely aggressive. Worst prognosis of all thyroid cancers. Rapid fatal course.

🤚 Thyroiditis

Palpation Thyroiditis

Vigorous clinical palpation causes mechanical trauma to follicles → morphological changes similar to subacute granulomatous thyroiditis.

Disease Timeline Pathological Progression
Goiter Evolution
Stage 1 — Iodine Deficiency

↓ T3/T4 → loss of negative feedback → excessive TSH secretion

Stage 2 — Diffuse Hyperplasia

TSH drives uniform follicular cell hypertrophy & hyperplasia → Simple goiter (euthyroid)

Stage 3 — Involution

T3/T4 restored → TSH falls → follicles involute with colloid accumulation

Stage 4 — Nodule Formation

Repeated cycles → fibrosis, hemorrhage, calcification, cysts → multinodular goiter

Stage 5 — Toxic MNG

Autonomous nodule becomes hyperfunctioning → hyperthyroidism

Hashimoto Progression
Stage 1 — Sensitization

CD4+ T cells become sensitized to thyroid antigens (thyroglobulin, TPO)

Stage 2 — Recruitment

CD4+ cells recruit CD8+ cytotoxic T cells and B lymphocytes

Stage 3 — Antibody Production

B cells produce anti-thyroglobulin + anti-TPO antibodies

Stage 4 — Follicular Destruction

Combined T-cell cytotoxicity + antibody-mediated damage → follicle death

Stage 5 — Hypothyroidism

Hurthle cell metaplasia, lymphoid follicles, atrophy → clinical hypothyroidism

⚠️ Exam Pearl — The Golden Rule of Follicular Lesions: Follicular Adenoma and Follicular Carcinoma are histologically identical in architecture. The ONLY differentiating criterion is the presence of capsular invasion and/or vascular invasion — which confirms carcinoma. No invasion = benign adenoma.

Diagnostic Algorithm — Thyroid Nodule Workup
Treatment Overview Management
🔴 Graves' Disease (Hyperthyroidism)
🚨 Thyroid Storm

Beta-blockers (propranolol) + PTU + steroids + iodide (Wolff-Chaikoff). ICU admission.

Antithyroid Drugs

Propylthiouracil (PTU) or Methimazole — block thyroid peroxidase → ↓ hormone synthesis

Radioiodine (¹³¹I)

Destroys hyperfunctioning tissue. First-line in many patients. May cause hypothyroidism.

Surgical

Subtotal or total thyroidectomy. Used if large goiter, compression, or failed medical therapy.

Beta-Blockers

Propranolol for symptomatic relief (palpitations, tremor, anxiety) while awaiting definitive therapy.

Exophthalmos

Selenium, steroids, orbital decompression if severe. Does NOT always improve after thyroid treatment.

🔵 Hashimoto Thyroiditis (Hypothyroidism)
Levothyroxine (T4)

Lifelong replacement therapy. Dosing based on weight, age, TSH target.

Monitoring

Regular TSH + FT4 levels every 6–12 months once stable. Anti-TPO titres for initial confirmation.

Autoimmune Workup

Screen for associated autoimmune diseases (Type 1 DM, celiac, Addison's, vitiligo).

🟡 Thyroiditis Management
De Quervain

NSAIDs or aspirin for pain. Steroids if severe. Self-limiting — full recovery expected. Monitor TFTs.

Riedel Thyroiditis

Tamoxifen (anti-fibrotic). Steroids may help. Surgery if severe compression. Unusual/rare entity.

Hashimoto Flares

Monitor TFTs; treat hypothyroidism with levothyroxine; no specific immunotherapy recommended.

🟢 Goiter
Iodine Supplementation

Endemic goiter prevention via iodised salt. Effective for simple diffuse goitre if caught early.

TSH Suppression

Levothyroxine to suppress TSH and reduce goitre size. Limited efficacy for nodular goitre.

Surgery

Thyroidectomy for large compressive MNG, substernal extension, malignancy concern, or cosmesis.

Radioiodine

For toxic MNG (Plummer's disease) — reduces volume and controls hyperthyroidism.

🔴 Thyroid Carcinomas
Papillary / Follicular

Total thyroidectomy + ¹³¹I ablation + lifelong levothyroxine (suppress TSH to prevent recurrence).

Medullary

Total thyroidectomy + central neck dissection. Screen family (RET testing). ¹³¹I NOT effective (not follicular origin).

Anaplastic

No curative treatment. Palliative chemo/radiotherapy. Median survival weeks to months.
