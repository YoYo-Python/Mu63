Topic Overview

Three core topics — all high-yield anatomy for your exam

⚠️
EXAM CRITICAL: "Water Under the Bridge" — ureter passes INFERIOR to uterine artery at lateral cervix. Most tested surgical anatomy in this topic.
🥚

Topic 1: The Ovary

Primary Sex Organ
  • Attached to broad ligament via mesovarium
  • Ovarian fossa boundaries (3 walls)
  • Artery: abdominal aorta → IVC/left renal
  • Ureter: posterior to ovary
🔴

Topic 2: Uterus & Support

High Yield ⭐
  • Fundus → Body → Cervix
  • Position: anteverted + anteflexed
  • 3 key ligaments prevent prolapse
  • Uterine a. crosses over ureter ⚠️
💙

Topic 3: Vagina, Vulva & Urethra

Clinical
  • Vestibule: urethral → vaginal → Bartholin
  • Urethra: 4 cm long, 2 sphincters
  • Vagina: ant 7 cm / post 8.5–9 cm
  • Pain: lower 1/3 only (pudendal nerve)

⚠️ High-Yield Exam Points

1. Water Under the Bridge: Uterine artery runs ANTERIOR & SUPERIOR to ureter. The ureter is inferior. During hysterectomy → risk of ureteric ligation → anuria.
2. Left vs Right Ovarian Vein: Right → IVC directly. Left → Left renal vein (then IVC). Clinically significant in left-sided varicocele-like presentation.
3. Pain-free upper vagina: Upper 2/3 innervated by autonomic uterovaginal plexus → insensitive to somatic pain. Lower 1/3 → pudendal nerve → pain-sensitive.
4. Three pillar ligaments of uterus: Transverse cervical (cardinal) + Pubocervical + Uterosacral ligaments = primary ligamentous support against prolapse.
5. Fertilization site: Ampulla of Fallopian tube (lateral 2/3, widest part). Implantation here = ectopic pregnancy.
6. Lymph drainage of fundus: Follows ovarian artery → para-aortic & lateral aortic nodes (NOT internal iliac).
7. Round ligament: Uterus → inguinal canal → labium majus. Stretches in pregnancy → round ligament pain in groin.

⚕️ Indication: Severe uterine bleeding → Decision to perform hysterectomy
↓
Surgeon ligates the uterine artery (from internal iliac artery)
↓
⚠️ Danger zone: Uterine artery crosses ANTERIOR to ureter at lateral cervix ("water under the bridge")
↓
❌ Ureter inadvertently clamped/ligated instead of (or along with) the uterine artery
↓
Urine cannot flow from kidney → ureter → bladder
↓

↓
