Overview

High-yield summary of Gonadal Development & Anomalies

🔍
⚠️ Exam Pearl: SRY → TDF → Testis development. Absence of SRY → ovary by default. This is the single most tested concept in this module!
🧬
Core Concept
Sex Determination
Genetic sex (XY or XX) is set at fertilization. The SRY gene on the Y chromosome encodes Testis Determining Factor (TDF). Development begins at week 4 and phenotypical differentiation is complete by week 20.
Exam Favourite High-Yield
📍
Week 4-6
Genital Ridge Formation
Condensation of intermediate mesoderm + celomic epithelium. PGCs migrate from yolk sac endoderm → along dorsal mesentery of hindgut → invade genital ridges. PGCs provide the inductive stimulus.
High-Yield Mechanism
🔬
Cellular Origins — Testis
Who Makes What?
🟦 PGCs → Spermatogonia
🟨 Celomic epithelium → Sertoli cells (+ MIF)
🟥 Intermediate mesoderm → Leydig cells (+ testosterone) & tunica albuginea
Exam Favourite Clinical Pearl
🥚
Ovary Development
Cortical Cords → Follicles
No SRY = no TDF. Primitive sex cords degenerate → vascular stroma (medulla). 2nd-gen cortical cords form → fragment into isolated clusters → Primordial follicles (PGC = oogonium; celomic cell = follicular cell).
Exam Favourite

Congenital Anomaly
Cryptorchidism
Arrested descent along normal path. Causes: hormonal failure, gubernacular failure, ↓ intra-abdominal pressure. Risks: sterility, malignancy, atrophy. Very, very, very common!
Exam Favourite Clinical Pearl
🔀
Congenital Anomaly
Ectopic Testis
Deviates from normal path due to abnormally strong band of gubernaculum. Most common site: superficial fascia of anterior abdominal wall. Distinguish from cryptorchidism by location!
High-Yield
🔄
Intersex Conditions
Hermaphroditism
True: Both ovarian + testicular tissue
Male Pseudo (AIS): XY but receptor insensitivity → female phenotype
Female Pseudo (CAH): XX but adrenal androgen excess → male phenotype
Exam Favourite High-Yield
💧
Processus Vaginalis
Patent PV Complications
Normally obliterates after descent. If patent: peritoneal fluid → congenital hydrocele; intestinal loop → congenital inguinal hernia. Distal remnant = tunica vaginalis (normal).
Clinical Pearl
🔗
Female Anatomy
Gubernaculum in Females
Attaches to the side of the uterus → halts ovarian descent in the pelvis. Becomes: ovarian ligament (upper) + round ligament of uterus (lower). Ovary stays in pelvis, not labia.
High-Yield
🧅

🟡 Cremasteric muscle ← internal oblique

Exam Favourite
🌡️
Physiology
Why the Scrotum?
Spermatogenesis requires ~33°C (vs body's 37°C). This is the primary reason the testes MUST descend to the scrotum. Retained testes → infertility due to elevated temperature.

🎯 High-Yield Summary: The SRY gene → TDF → male gonadal development. PGCs from yolk sac endoderm form the inductive trigger. Sertoli cells (celomic epi) secrete MIF. Leydig cells (mesoderm) secrete testosterone. In females, no TDF → cortical cords → primordial follicles. Gubernaculum guides descent (testis to scrotum) or becomes ovarian/round ligaments (female).
