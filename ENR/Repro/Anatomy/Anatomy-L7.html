TOPIC 01

🪣 Ischiorectal Fossa

Wedge-shaped fat-filled space lateral to anal canal. Key site of abscess and pudendal canal.

TOPIC 02

📦 Perineal Pouches

Deep (closed) vs Superficial (open anteriorly). Gland locations and fascial spread of urine.

TOPIC 03

🏛 Perineal Body

Central fibromuscular pillar anchoring pelvic floor. Disruption → organ prolapse in females.

⚡ Exam Flags — Highest Yield
⚠️Inferior rectal nerve and vessels traverse the ischiorectal fossa from lateral to medial.
⚠️Ischiorectal abscess → cruciate incision at most fluctuant point; if untreated → anal/cutaneous fistula.
⚠️Bulbourethral gland body is in the DEEP pouch; its DUCT pierces the perineal membrane to open in the SUPERFICIAL pouch.
⚠️Urethral rupture in superficial pouch → urine tracks anteriorly under Scarpa's fascia on abdominal wall (NOT into thighs or anal triangle).
⚠️Greater vestibular gland (female) is in the SUPERFICIAL perineal pouch.
⚠️Damage to perineal body during labour → prolapse of uterus; episiotomy controls tearing.

🗺 Perineum Boundaries
◆Anterior angle: Symphysis pubis (lower border)
◆Posterior angle: Tip of coccyx
◆Lateral angles: Ischial tuberosities
◆Divided by: Imaginary transverse line between ischial tuberosities → Urogenital triangle (anterior) + Anal triangle (posterior)
◆Location: Below pelvic diaphragm, overlying pelvic outlet
