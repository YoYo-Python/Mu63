Biochemistry of Sex Hormones

Sex hormones are steroid hormones derived from cholesterol. They are produced by the gonads (testes & ovaries) and the adrenal cortex. They act via genomic (slow) and non-genomic (rapid) pathways. Master the biosynthesis pathway, structural features, and mechanisms to ace your exam.

⚠️ Adrenal CORTEX not medulla StAR = rate-limiting step FSH controls aromatase in granulosa
⚠️

EXAM ALERT: Always write Adrenal CORTEX — never adrenal medulla. The medulla produces catecholamines, not steroid hormones. This was explicitly highlighted by your lecturer as a common mistake.

🗂️ Classification
⚡

Strong Androgens

Testosterone (19C) and DHT (more powerful). DHT formed peripherally by 5α-reductase from testosterone.

🌿

Weak Androgens

DHEA and Androstenedione — precursors in the steroidogenesis pathway.

🌸

Estrogens

E1 (postmenopausal), E2 Estradiol (most powerful, pre-menopause, 18C), E3 Estriol (pregnancy).

🤰

Progestogens

Progesterone (21C) — main product of the luteal phase. Maintains pregnancy and prevents uterine contractions.

🏭

Sites of Production

Gonads (ovaries, testes) and Adrenal Cortex. Peripheral: fat cells, bone, skin.

🧬

Nature

All are lipid-soluble steroid hormones with 4 fused rings (A, B, C, D) derived from cholesterol.

🔑 Key Points Grid
🏃 Rate-Limiting Step

StAR protein transfers cholesterol to the inner mitochondrial membrane, where P450scc cleaves it to Pregnenolone. This is the rate-limiting step in all tissues. Stimulated by LH via cAMP.

High Yield
🔄 Two-Cell Model (Ovary)

Granulosa cells make pregnenolone → diffuses to theca cells → becomes androstenedione → returns to granulosa → aromatase (FSH-controlled) → estrone → estradiol.

Two-Cell System
🧠 Genomic Pathway

Lipid-soluble hormones cross membrane freely → bind cytoplasm/nuclear receptor → complex enters nucleus → binds DNA response element in gene promoter → acts as transcription factor (ON/OFF).

Slow pathway
🌙 Postmenopausal Estrogen

After menopause, ovaries stop producing E2. Estrone (E1) becomes dominant — produced by peripheral aromatization in fat cells, bone, and adrenal gland. Explains why obese women have higher estrogen levels.

Clinical Pearl

👩 Female Pathway (Two-Cell Model)

Granulosa Cells (LH-initiated)

LH → StAR/P450scc → Cholesterol → Pregnenolone → diffuses to theca cells

Theca Cells (via 3β-HSD)

Pregnenolone → 17α-hydroxylase/17,20-lyase → DHEA → 3β-HSD → Androstenedione → returns to granulosa cells

Back in Granulosa Cells (FSH-controlled)

Androstenedione → Aromatase → Estrone → 17β-HSD → Estradiol (E2)

FSH controls the expression of both Aromatase and 17β-HSD in granulosa cells — this is why FSH drives follicular estradiol production.
