🔬 Testis — At a Glance

The testis is a compound tubular gland with both exocrine (spermatozoa) and endocrine (testosterone) functions. Highly tested in practical and written exams.

Tunica Albuginea

White fibrous capsule. Thickens posteriorly → Mediastinum Testis

🔲
Septa / Trabeculae

Divide testis into ~250 lobules. Each lobule contains 1–4 seminiferous tubules

Part of stroma. Support the seminiferous tubules

Tunica Vaginalis

Outer serous sac (peritoneal covering)

⚠️ Exam Pearl

"Parts of stroma = Tunica albuginea + Septa + Reticular fibers"

🏥 Sertoli Cells
⚠️ Very High Yield
📐
Morphology

Tall columnar, irregular lateral surface, rest on basement membrane

🔒
Tight Junctions → BTB

Continuous TJs between lateral borders = Blood-Testis Barrier

🧪
Secrete ABP

Androgen-Binding Protein concentrates testosterone inside tubules

🗑️
Phagocytosis

Abundant lysosomes digest excess cytoplasm shed during spermiogenesis

⚠️ EM Exam

EM hallmarks: Lysosomes + Tight Junctions → function = Blood-Testis Barrier

⚗️ Leydig Cells
Endocrine
📍
Location

Interstitial tissue between seminiferous tubules

🔵
LM Appearance

Large polygonal cells with acidophilic vacuolated cytoplasm

🔬
EM Features (Steroid-secreting)

Numerous lipid droplets, tubular cristae mitochondria, abundant sER

🧬
Function

Synthesize testosterone hormone

⚠️ EM Exam

Any steroid-secreting cell = lipid droplets + sER + tubular cristae mitochondria

🔄 Spermiogenesis
⚠️ MOST Tested
🟡
Golgi → Acrosome

Forms acrosomal vesicle covering anterior 2/3 of nucleus. Contains hydrolytic enzymes for fertilization

🌀
Centriole → Flagellum

One centriole attaches to nucleus; other elongates to form axoneme

⚡
Mitochondria → Middle Piece Sheath

Aggregate around proximal flagellum = energy for motility

🗑️
Nucleus Condenses + Cytoplasm Shed

Excess cytoplasm phagocytosed by Sertoli lysosomes

⚠️ Triple Exam Hit

Golgi→Acrosome · Centriole→Flagellum · Mitochondria→Middle Sheath

🛡️ Blood-Testis Barrier
Essay Topic
🔗
Structure

Continuous tight junctions between lateral borders of adjacent Sertoli cells

📦
Two Compartments

Basal (spermatogonia, early spermatocytes) vs Adluminal (post-meiotic cells)

🔄
Dynamic Movement

TJs disassemble ABOVE and reassemble BELOW moving primary spermatocytes

🛡️
Significance

Isolates haploid cells from host immune system → prevents autoimmune infertility
