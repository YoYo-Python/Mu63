Topic Overview

Histological structure of intra/extra-testicular ducts, accessory glands & penis

🔍
⚠️ Exam Focus #1: Vasa efferentia — alternating patches of simple cubical (non-ciliated) + simple columnar (ciliated) epithelium — the "patchy" appearance is the #1 identifying feature.
⚠️ Exam Focus #2: Any opening to the outside → STRATIFIED SQUAMOUS. Terminal penile urethra = stratified squamous epithelium.
⚠️ Exam Focus #3: Peripheral zone (75%) = Prostatic Carcinoma site. Transition zone (5%) = BPH site.
🔵
Tubuli Recti

Simple Cubical Epithelium

Proximal: Sertoli cells. Distal: simple cubical. Straight tubules connecting seminiferous tubules → rete testis.

🕸
Rete Testis

Simple Cubical Epithelium

Narrow branching anastomosing tubules in mediastinum testis. Network collects sperm from all seminiferous tubules.

🔀
Vasa Efferentia ⭐ EXAM

Alternating Patchy Epithelium

Simple cubical (non-ciliated) + simple columnar (ciliated) in patches. Cilia sweep sperm; non-ciliated cells absorb fluid.

🫀
Epididymis

Pseudostratified + Stereocilia

Pseudostratified columnar with long stereocilia (microvilli). Storage, biochemical maturation, phagocytosis of residual bodies.

💪
Vas Deferens

Thick 3-Layer Musculosa

Inner longitudinal → Middle circular → Outer longitudinal. Powerful peristaltic ejaculation. Pseudostratified columnar with stereocilia.

🔗
Ejaculatory Duct

Pseudo/Simple Columnar

Formed in prostate by union of vas deferens ampulla + seminal vesicle duct. Carries sperm into prostatic urethra.

🌀
Seminal Vesicles

Tortuous Tubular Gland

Pseudostratified/simple columnar. Secretes 70% of semen: fructose (energy) + fibrinogen (coagulation). Folded mucosa.

⭕
Prostate Gland ⭐ EXAM

Tubuloacinar Glands

3 zones: Transition (5%=BPH) / Central (20%) / Peripheral (75%=Carcinoma). Secretes 25-30% of semen; proteolytic enzymes liquefy clot.

💧
Bulbourethral Glands

Mixed Epithelium

Simple squamous, cubical, or columnar. Located under membranous urethra. Secretes mucus for urethral lubrication.

🔴
Corpora Cavernosa (×2)

Dorsal Erectile Tissue

Two dorsal masses. Vascular spaces lined by endothelium. Surrounded by thick tunica albuginea (dense CT).

🟡
Corpus Spongiosum (×1)

Ventral — Contains Urethra

Single ventral erectile mass. Contains the penile (spongy) urethra within. Thinner tunica albuginea than corpora cavernosa.

↔️
Male Urethra

3 Segments

Prostatic (3-4 cm, transitional) → Membranous (1 cm, shortest) → Penile/Spongy (15 cm, ends stratified squamous).

Sperm Transport Pathway

Click each step to expand epithelial details and functional adaptations

▶

1. Seminiferous Tubules (Testis)

Origin of spermatogenesis. Lined by Sertoli + spermatogenic cells.

Sertoli cells provide support, nutrition, and form the blood-testis barrier. Spermatids undergo spermiogenesis here — final differentiation into spermatozoa with flagella.
▶

2. Tubuli Recti (Straight Tubules)

Proximal: Sertoli → Distal: Simple Cubical

Short, straight tubules. The proximal segment retains Sertoli cells. The distal segment transitions to simple cuboidal epithelium with no cilia. Primary function: passive transport.
▶

3. Rete Testis

Simple Cubical Epithelium — Mediastinum testis

Narrow branching and anastomosing tubules in the mediastinum testis. Functions as a collecting network — all seminiferous tubule output converges here. No secretory or absorptive function.
▶

4. Vasa Efferentia ⭐ HIGH YIELD

PATCHY: Simple Cubical (non-cil.) + Simple Columnar (cil.)

KEY EXAM FACT: The alternating patches of tall ciliated columnar cells and short non-ciliated cubical cells creates the unique undulating/patchy appearance. Ciliated cells mechanically sweep immature, non-motile sperm forward. Non-ciliated cells absorb excess testicular fluid (concentrated sperm). ~15-20 ductules piercing the tunica albuginea.
▶

5. Epididymis (Head → Body → Tail)

Pseudostratified Columnar + Stereocilia

Functions: (1) Sperm storage (mainly tail) (2) Biochemical maturation — sperm acquire motility potential here over ~12 days (3) Phagocytosis of residual cytoplasmic bodies extruded during spermiogenesis. Stereocilia = long non-motile microvilli (NOT cilia). Surrounded by smooth muscle (increases in body/tail).
▶

6. Vas Deferens (Ductus Deferens)

Thick Musculosa: Inner Long. / Middle Circ. / Outer Long.

Pseudostratified columnar epithelium with stereocilia. The defining feature is its thick, 3-layered musculosa — enables rapid, powerful peristaltic contractions during ejaculation. Travels in spermatic cord through inguinal canal. Ampulla = dilated terminal portion that joins with seminal vesicle duct.
▶

7. Ejaculatory Duct (within Prostate)

Pseudostratified or Simple Columnar

Formed by union of vas deferens ampulla + seminal vesicle duct INSIDE the prostate gland. No muscularis of its own — ejaculation relies on prostate smooth muscle + vas deferens. Opens into prostatic urethra at the verumontanum.
▶

8. Prostatic Urethra → Membranous → Penile

Transitional → Stratified Columnar → Stratified Squamous (terminal)

Prostatic (3-4 cm): Transitional epithelium. Membranous (1 cm): Stratified columnar. Penile/Spongy (15 cm): Stratified/pseudostratified columnar, transitions to STRATIFIED SQUAMOUS at the external urethral meatus. Rule: anything opening to outside = stratified squamous.

⚡ Coagulation vs Liquefaction: Seminal vesicle fibrinogen → coagulates semen (keeps it in vagina). Prostate proteolytic enzymes → liquefies clot (releases motile sperm). If prostate fails → semen stays as clot = infertility.
⚡ Stereocilia ≠ Cilia: Stereocilia are LONG MICROVILLI (non-motile) found on epididymis and vas deferens epithelium. The moving cilia in vasa efferentia are TRUE CILIA on the columnar cells.
⚡ Corpora amylacea: Normal age-related finding in prostate. Calcified dried secretions within acinar lumens. Do NOT confuse with carcinoma.
