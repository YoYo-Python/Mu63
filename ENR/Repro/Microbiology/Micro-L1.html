🗺️ Overview

Four pillars of this lecture — normal flora, Gonorrhea, Syphilis, and Chlamydia

🌸
Normal Vaginal Flora
Doderlein's Bacilli — Protective Commensals
⚠️ Exam Protective
  • 🧪Aerobic Lactobacilli ferment glycogen → lactic acid → acidic pH
  • 🛡️Inhibits Staphylococci, Streptococci, and Candida albicans
  • ⚠️Absent pre-puberty; diminishes post-menopause — only during reproductive life
🔴
Neisseria gonorrhoeae
Gonorrhea — STI
🔥 High-Yield ⚠️ Exam
  • 🔬Gram-negative, kidney-shaped diplococci — intracellular in neutrophils
  • 🍫Chocolate agar (enriched) or Thayer-Martin (selective), 10% CO₂, 37°C
  • 🧬Ferments glucose ONLY (not maltose); Oxidase positive
  • ⚠️NO capsule — unlike N. meningitidis
🌀
Treponema pallidum
Syphilis — STI
🔥 High-Yield Spirochete
  • 🌀Slender spirochete — axial filaments for corkscrew motility
  • ⚠️Cannot be cultured in vitro — maintained in rabbit testes (Nichol's strain)
  • 🔍Visualized by dark-field microscopy or silver impregnation
  • 💊Penicillin remains the drug of choice for all stages
🔶
Chlamydia trachomatis
LGV, NGU — Obligate Intracellular
🔥 High-Yield Biphasic Life Cycle
  • ⚡Cannot make its own ATP — obligate intracellular pathogen
  • 🔄EB (infectious, extracellular) ↔ RB (replicating, intracellular)
  • ⚠️Penicillin NOT effective — use Tetracycline or Erythromycin
  • 🎯LGV serovars: L1, L2, L3 — cause buboes

📅 Syphilis Disease Timeline

Primary Syphilis
Painless, indurated (hard) chancre at the site of inoculation. Highly contagious. Regional lymphadenopathy. Heals spontaneously without treatment.
Secondary Syphilis
Systemic spread via blood. Widespread mucocutaneous rash. Condylomata (moist, wart-like lesions) in intertriginous areas. Highly contagious. VDRL positive.
Latent Syphilis
Asymptomatic but seropositive. Organism persists in tissues. May reactivate. Not contagious via skin/mucosa (but can still cross placenta).
Tertiary Syphilis
Gummas (granulomatous-like destructive lesions). Cardiovascular syphilis (aortitis, aneurysms). Neurosyphilis (tabes dorsalis, general paresis). Irreversible damage.
Congenital Syphilis
Transplacental transmission. Hutchinson teeth, saddle-nose deformity, interstitial keratitis. Early (2 yrs) vs Late congenital forms.

🔶 Chlamydia Diagnostic Methods

⚠️ Key Exam Points — Serology

  • ⚠️Wassermann: Absence of hemolysis = POSITIVE (complement fixed by reagin antibodies)
  • ⚠️False positives: VDRL/RPR can be positive in SLE, malaria, TB, pregnancy (non-specific cardiolipin antibodies)
  • ✅FTA-ABS remains positive for life even after successful treatment — not useful for treatment monitoring
  • 📉VDRL/RPR titers fall with successful treatment — useful for monitoring
  • 🔍Dark-field microscopy is the GOLD STANDARD for visualizing T. pallidum from primary chancre

💊 Treatment

Color-coded by organism — know your drugs and contraindications

🔴 Gonorrhea (N. gonorrhoeae)

First-Line Treatment
Recommended dual therapy to cover co-infection with Chlamydia and address increasing antibiotic resistance:
Ceftriaxone (IM) + Azithromycin
Special Neonatal Case — Ophthalmia Neonatorum
Prophylaxis: 1% silver nitrate eye drops OR erythromycin ointment applied to neonatal eyes immediately after birth. Systemic ceftriaxone for established infection.
Silver Nitrate 1% (prophylaxis) Ceftriaxone (treatment)

🌀 Syphilis (T. pallidum)

Penicillin — All Clinical Stages
Penicillin G (Benzathine Penicillin IM) remains the drug of choice for ALL stages of syphilis, including primary, secondary, latent, tertiary, and congenital syphilis. Penicillin works by disrupting cell wall synthesis (T. pallidum has a cell wall unlike Chlamydia).
Benzathine Penicillin G Doxycycline (if penicillin allergic)

🔶 Chlamydia (C. trachomatis)

Drugs of Choice
Tetracycline and Erythromycin are the primary agents. Azithromycin (single dose) is also highly effective for NGU and genital infections. These drugs penetrate intracellular environments to reach the Reticulate body.
Tetracycline Erythromycin Azithromycin Doxycycline
Penicillin
Penicillin is explicitly NOT effective against Chlamydia. Chlamydia lacks a conventional peptidoglycan cell wall, the target of penicillin, and resides intracellularly beyond penicillin's reach in effective concentrations.
Penicillin ❌ Contraindicated
