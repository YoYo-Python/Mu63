TORCH Infections — Overview

Four major congenital viral pathogens and their clinical significance

⚠️ EXAM HIGH-YIELD ALERTS
  • CMV periventricular calcifications → pathognomonic on fetal ultrasound
  • CRS classic triad → Sensorineural Deafness + Cataracts + Congenital Heart Defect (PDA)
  • SNHL is the most common single feature of BOTH CMV and CRS
  • Parvovirus B19 is NOT a teratogen — causes hematological damage only
  • Live-attenuated vaccines (MMR, Varivax) are CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
  • Neonatal Varicella critical window: maternal rash 5 days before → 2 days after delivery
  • MCA peak systolic velocity ↑ on Doppler = non-invasive sign of fetal anemia (Parvo B19)
🦠 Cytomegalovirus (CMV) — HHV-5 Beta-herpesvirus
  • Genome: Linear dsDNA, ~235 kb — largest human herpesvirus
  • Latency site: Monocytes, macrophages, renal tubular cells
  • Transmission: Saliva, sexual contact, vertical (transplacental), blood products
  • Reactivation: Asymptomatic shedding in saliva/urine (healthy) → end-organ disease (immunocompromised) — retinitis, colitis
  • Congenital CID triad: Microcephaly + Periventricular calcifications + Sensorineural hearing loss
  • Pathognomonic finding in culture: Swollen "Owl-eye" intranuclear inclusions
  • Most common congenital viral infection
  • Delayed SNHL: 10–15% of asymptomatic congenital CMV infants develop SNHL by school age
  • Diagnosis: CMV PCR in urine/blood; amniocentesis (after 21 weeks + ≥6 weeks post infection)
🫙 Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV) — HHV-3 Alpha-herpesvirus
  • Genome: Linear dsDNA, ~125 kb; enveloped
  • Latency site: Dorsal root ganglia (sensory neurons)
  • Primary infection: Chickenpox (Varicella) — respiratory droplets
  • Reactivation: Herpes Zoster (Shingles) — anterograde, unilateral dermatomal vesicular rash
  • Congenital Varicella Syndrome (CVS) — <20 weeks: Limb hypoplasia, cicatricial skin scarring, muscle atrophy (due to fetal sensory ganglia infection → denervation)
  • Neonatal Varicella (critical window): Maternal rash −5 days to +2 days from delivery → no passive IgG transfer → pneumonia, hepatitis, encephalitis, 30% mortality
  • Infantile Herpes Zoster: VZV latency in fetal ganglia → shingles in first 12 months of life
  • Vaccine: Varivax (live-attenuated) — contraindicated in pregnancy
🩸 Parvovirus B19 Autonomous Parvovirus
  • Genome: Linear ssDNA — smallest human pathogenic virus; non-enveloped
  • Target cell: Erythroid progenitor cells (requires mitotically active cells)
  • Replication pathway: Nasopharynx → primary viremia → bone marrow → massive secondary viremia → placenta, joints, skin
  • NOT a teratogen — causes hematological damage ONLY
  • Hydrops Fetalis: Fetal RBC progenitor destruction → severe fetal anemia → fetal heart failure → fluid in ≥2 compartments (ascites, pleural/pericardial effusion, skin edema)
  • Erythema Infectiosum (5th Disease): "Slapped-cheek" rash, lacy reticular trunk rash in children; rash appears day 14–21 (immunologic phase, after peak viremia)
  • Transient Aplastic Crisis: In sickle cell, thalassemia, spherocytosis — sudden severe Hgb drop
  • Chronic Pure Red Cell Aplasia: In immunocompromised — ongoing progenitor destruction
  • Maternal diagnosis: IgM (recent infection), PCR for B19 DNA
  • Fetal monitoring: MCA peak systolic velocity ↑ (non-invasive anemia marker)
  • Treatment: Intrauterine blood transfusion (IUT) via cordocentesis for severe fetal anemia
  • No vaccine available
🌹 Rubella Virus Rubivirus (Togaviridae)
  • Genome: Positive-sense ssRNA; enveloped with hemagglutinin spikes
  • Only member of genus Rubivirus (Matonaviridae)
  • Replication pathway: Oropharynx → local lymph nodes → viremia (day 5–7) → internal organs, placenta, skin
  • Classic TERATOGEN — risk by trimester: 0–12 wks ≈ 90% | 13–16 wks ≈ 10–20% | >16 wks ≈ low
  • CRS Classic Triad: Sensorineural Deafness (most common) + Cataracts + Congenital Heart Defects (PDA / branch pulmonary artery stenosis)
  • Other CRS features: Blueberry muffin rash (dermal extramedullary hematopoiesis), hepatosplenomegaly, microcephaly
  • Healthy child presentation: Pink maculopapular rash spreading head → foot, posterior auricular lymphadenopathy, low-grade fever
  • Prevention: MMR vaccine (live-attenuated) — give >1 month before conception; CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
  • Diagnosis: Serology (IgM/IgG), RT-PCR most sensitive in viremic phase (before rash)

🔭 Diagnostic Algorithm — Pregnant Woman with Suspected TORCH Infection
1
Clinical Assessment + Exposure History
Identify rash type (slapped-cheek → B19; maculopapular descending → Rubella; vesicular dermatomal → VZV; absent → CMV). Document gestational age, maternal illness, travel, vaccine history.
2
Maternal Serology (IgM + IgG)
IgM positive = acute/recent infection. Paired IgG titres (acute + convalescent) showing ≥4× rise = recent seroconversion. Note: IgM can be false positive in certain conditions.
3
PCR Confirmation in Mother
Send pathogen-specific PCR from blood/nasopharynx. Most sensitive during viremic phase (before rash appears). CMV: urine PCR. B19: blood DNA. VZV: vesicle PCR.
4
Fetal Monitoring — Serial Ultrasound
Assess every 1–2 weeks for: periventricular calcifications (CMV), limb hypoplasia (VZV), hydrops fetalis / increased MCA peak systolic velocity (B19), structural cardiac anomalies (Rubella), growth restriction.
5
Invasive Fetal Sampling (if indicated)
Amniocentesis (≥21 weeks, ≥6 weeks post-infection): CMV PCR in AF — fetal kidneys must excrete virus. Cordocentesis (PUBS): fetal CBC for anemia (B19) + pathogen PCR. Enables IUT for B19 hydrops.
6
Neonatal Workup (≤21 days of age)
CMV: urine PCR within 3 weeks (gold standard — differentiates congenital from postnatal). Hearing screening (SNHL — CMV & Rubella). Ophthalmology (cataracts — Rubella; chorioretinitis — CMV). Cardiac echo (PDA — Rubella).

Treatment & Prevention

Management strategies for each congenital viral infection

🦠 Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  • Symptomatic neonates: IV Ganciclovir or oral Valganciclovir for 6 months — improves hearing outcomes and neurodevelopment
  • Immunocompromised adults (reactivation): Ganciclovir / Valganciclovir; Foscarnet if resistance; Cidofovir as last resort
  • CMV retinitis: Intravitreal Ganciclovir implant + oral Valganciclovir
  • Prevention: Universal newborn hearing screening; serial audiological follow-up to school age (delayed SNHL)
  • No licensed CMV vaccine available currently
  • Prophylaxis in transplant patients: Oral Valganciclovir (pre-emptive or universal strategy)
🫙 Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV)
  • Maternal chickenpox: Oral Acyclovir (initiate within 24h of rash)
  • Neonatal Varicella (critical window): IV Acyclovir immediately; Varicella-Zoster Immune Globulin (VZIG) prophylactically to neonate
  • Herpes Zoster: Oral Valacyclovir or Famciclovir (7–10 days)
  • Prevention — Varivax (live-attenuated): 2 doses; given at 12–15 months + 4–6 years; CONTRAINDICATED IN PREGNANCY
  • Pre-conception: Screen seronegative women → vaccinate ≥1 month before conception
  • Congenital Varicella Syndrome: Supportive care; ophthalmology, physiotherapy, neurological follow-up
🩸 Parvovirus B19
  • Healthy children (5th Disease): Supportive care only — self-limiting (7–10 days)
  • Fetal hydrops: Intrauterine Blood Transfusion (IUT) via cordocentesis — significantly improves fetal survival
  • Monitoring fetal anemia: Serial MCA Doppler ultrasound (PSV >1.5 MoM = significant anemia)
  • Transient Aplastic Crisis: RBC transfusion support until immune clearance
  • Chronic Pure Red Cell Aplasia (immunocompromised): IV Immunoglobulin (IVIG) — reduces viral load
  • No vaccine available
  • Polyarthralgia in adults: NSAIDs for symptom relief
🌹 Rubella Virus
  • Maternal Rubella: No antiviral treatment — supportive care only
  • Congenital Rubella Syndrome: No cure — management of sequelae
  • SNHL: Hearing aids / cochlear implants
  • Cataracts: Early surgical removal; refractive correction
  • Cardiac defects (PDA): Indomethacin (medical closure) or surgical ligation / catheter-based closure
  • Prevention — MMR vaccine (live-attenuated): 2 doses at 12–15 months + 4–6 years; CONTRAINDICATED IN PREGNANCY
  • Pre-conception: Rubella IgG screening → vaccinate seronegative women ≥1 month before conception
