📖 Topic Overview

Parasites of the Reproductive System — from ectoparasites to oncogenesis.

🔍

⚠️ HIGH-YIELD EXAM CONCEPT

Both T. vaginalis and S. haematobium dramatically increase cervical cancer risk through chronic inflammation that facilitates HPV acquisition and prevents viral clearance.

💡 Always treat BOTH partners for T. vaginalis — males are often asymptomatic (up to 50%) but can reinfect the female (ping-pong transmission).
🎓 Granuloma formation by S. haematobium is the pre-cancerous stage — called "الجرانيولوما دي البريكانس".
🦠 Parasite Categories

CATEGORY 01 — ARTHROPODS

Phthirus pubis & Sarcoptes scabiei
Ectoparasites affecting external genitalia. P. pubis attaches to pubic hair; S. scabiei creates intracutaneous tunnels. Cause severe itching, dermatitis, and secondary bacterial infections.
⚠️ EXAM: Stops at dermatitis Permethrin Rx

CATEGORY 02 — HELMINTHS

Schistosoma, Enterobius & W. bancrofti
Cause granulomatous lesions, obstructive fibrosis, and lymphatic obstruction. S. haematobium eggs trapped in pelvic venules cause granuloma. W. bancrofti obstructs lymphatics → elephantiasis.
🔥 Pre-cancerous granuloma Praziquantel Rx

CATEGORY 03 — PROTOZOA

T. vaginalis, T. gondii, Plasmodium & T. cruzi
Intracellular damage and cytotoxic cytolysis. T. vaginalis causes contact-dependent cytolysis. T. cruzi amastigotes cause irreversible nerve/muscle destruction → Mega uterus.
💊 Both partners! Metronidazole Rx

CATEGORY 04 — ONCOGENESIS

Parasite-Induced Cancer
T. vaginalis releases TvMIF and increases IL-6, facilitating HPV acquisition. S. haematobium causes cervical erosions (HPV entry) and interferes with viral clearance (HPV persistence).
🎯 MOST HIGH-YIELD Colposcopy screen
🔄 Cervical Cancer Pathway — Parasite to Malignancy
🦠
Step 1 — Parasite Infection

Sexual contact with T. vaginalis or skin penetration by S. haematobium furcocercous cercariae. Parasite establishes in genitourinary tract or pelvic venous plexus.

→ Infection established
↓
🔥
Step 2 — Chronic Inflammation

T. vaginalis releases pro-inflammatory cytokines + TvMIF + ↑IL-6. S. haematobium eggs trapped in vessel walls → granuloma + fibrosis → cervical erosions and ulcerations.

→ Epithelial barrier disrupted
↓
🦠
Step 3 — HPV Acquisition

Cervical erosions act as entry points for Human Papilloma Virus (HPV). Inflammatory environment facilitates viral adhesion and penetration.

→ HPV enters cervical epithelium
↓
🧬
Step 4 — Impaired Viral Clearance

Chronic schistosomal inflammation impairs local immune system from clearing HPV. TvMIF promotes abnormal cell proliferation. ↑ sustained viral load.

→ HPV persists & viral load rises
↓
🔬
Step 5 — Precancer & Cancer

HPV drives cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN) → cervical carcinoma. In males: T. vaginalis IL-6 ↑ → BPH → prostate cancer risk.

→ Cervical / Prostate Cancer
🎓 Quick-Reference Clinical Pearls
🍓
"Strawberry Cervix"

Classic sign of T. vaginalis — punctate hemorrhages on cervix giving a strawberry appearance. Also: greenish-yellow frothy discharge, foul smell.

⚠️ EXAM FAVORITE
🪨
"Sandy Patches"

Calcified S. haematobium egg deposits in bladder/cervix seen on colposcopy. Classic finding in female genital schistosomiasis + terminal hematuria.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD
🔴
"Mega Uterus"

T. cruzi amastigotes destroy nerve + muscle cells → massive uterine enlargement. Chronic stage — nerve destruction is IRREVERSIBLE even after treatment.

💎 CLINICAL PEARL
🦵
Scrotal Elephantiasis

W. bancrofti adult worms in lymph vessels → chronic obstruction → scrotal lymphedema, hydrocele, and elephantiasis. Endemic regions.

⚠️ MCQ TRAP
🐱
Congenital Toxoplasmosis

T. gondii transplacental transmission. In pregnancy → Spiramycin (NOT pyrimethamine). Source: cat feces (oocysts) OR undercooked meat (tissue cysts).

🔥 DRUG SWAP
🪱
Extra-intestinal Pinworm

Gravid E. vermicularis females migrate into vagina → uterus → fallopian tubes → granulomas, peritoneal adhesions, and tube-ovarian abscesses.

⚠️
EXAM TRAP: Female genital schistosomiasis (FGS) gold standard is biopsy of cervical tissue — NOT urine microscopy. Urine is for bladder schistosomiasis.
