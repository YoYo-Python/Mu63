Non-Neoplastic Cysts
Polycystic Ovary (PCOS / Stein-Leventhal)
Multiple cysts covered by dense fibrous capsule lined by luteinized theca cells. Elevated androgens → estrogens → unopposed estrogen → endometrial hyperplasia. Insulin resistance; young females; #1 cause of anovulatory infertility.
⚠️ Exam Fav
Follicular Cysts
Bilateral, multiple, clear fluid. Lined by inner granulosa + outer theca cells. Usually incidental; may → hyperestrogenism.
Corpus Luteum Cysts
Single, large; filled with blood or serous fluid. Lined by luteinized granulosa + outer theca. Can rupture → hemoperitoneum + acute abdomen.
Theca Lutein Cysts
Bilateral, multiple, large; lined by luteinized theca cells. Caused by elevated HCG. High association with gestational trophoblastic disease.
⚠️ Exam Fav
Endometriotic Cyst (Chocolate Cyst)
Dark brown contents. Endometrial glands + stroma in ovary undergo cyclical shedding → blood accumulates. Lined by hemosiderin-laden macrophages + endometrial glands/stroma.
⚠️ Chocolate = Blood = Hemosiderin
2
Surface Epithelial Tumors
Classification: Type I vs Type II
Type I: Low-grade, slow/indolent, good prognosis, from endometriosis/benign lesions.
Type II: High-grade, rapid/aggressive, poor prognosis, from STIC (fallopian tube).
⚠️ Core Concept
Malignancy Criterion
STROMAL INVASION is the ONLY feature distinguishing borderline from malignant. All other features (atypia, mitoses, multilayering) may appear in borderline tumors.
⚠️ #1 Exam Fact
Serous Cystadenoma
Clear watery fluid. Lined by single layer of tall, columnar, ciliated cells (like tubal epithelium). May have papillae + psammoma bodies.
Mucinous Cystadenoma
Huge multilocular cyst. Mucoid viscous material. Lined by single layer of bland mucinous epithelium (resembles endocervix/intestinal).
3
Germ Cell, Sex Cord & Metastatic
Dysgerminoma
Undifferentiated malignant germ cell tumor in children/young adults. Lobules of large polygonal glycogen-rich cells + fibrous septae infiltrated by lymphocytes. Analogue of testicular seminoma.
⚠️ = Seminoma (ovary)
Teratoma Types
Mature (Dermoid): Benign, cystic, from all 3 germ layers, no primitive elements.
Immature: Solid, malignant, primitive neuroepithelium.
Struma Ovarii: Monodermal → thyroid tissue → hyperthyroidism.
Fibroma + Meigs Syndrome
Pure stromal tumor. The triad: Ovarian Fibroma + Ascites + Right Hydrothorax = Meigs Syndrome. Benign; removal resolves effusions.
⚠️ Classic Triad
Krukenberg Tumor
Bilateral metastatic tumor. Trans-coelomic spread from primary GIT (mainly stomach). Signet-ring cells in fibrous stroma. May present with Sister Joseph's nodule at umbilicus.
⚠️ Signet Ring Cells!

🔄 PCOS Pathogenesis — Step-by-Step Flow
Step 1 — Trigger
Insulin resistance → ↑ insulin → stimulates ovarian theca cells
↓
Step 2 — Androgen Excess
↑ LH (pituitary) → ↑ theca cell androgen production (testosterone, DHEA)
↓
Step 3 — Peripheral Conversion
Androgens aromatized → estrogens (mainly in adipose tissue)
↓
Step 4 — Unopposed Estrogen
No ovulation → no corpus luteum → no progesterone → estrogen unopposed
↓
Step 5 — Ovarian Changes
Multiple follicles arrested in development → bilateral subcapsular follicular cysts with dense fibrous capsule
↓
Step 6 — Endometrial Consequence
Prolonged unopposed estrogen → endometrial hyperplasia → possible endometrial carcinoma risk
🔄 Krukenberg Tumor — Spread Mechanism
Primary Site
Gastric carcinoma (most common) — mucinous signet-ring cell type
↓
Route: Trans-coelomic Spread
Tumor cells exfoliate into peritoneal cavity → carried by peritoneal fluid
↓
Bilateral Ovarian Implantation
Cells implant on both ovarian surfaces → stromal reaction forms fibrous background
↓
Histology
Signet-ring cells (mucin-filled, nucleus pushed to periphery) within dense fibrous ovarian stroma
↓
Further Spread
Omentum, umbilicus (Sister Joseph's nodule), contralateral ovary

Diagnosis

Criteria · Investigations · Comparative tables · Diagnostic algorithms

🔑 Malignancy vs Borderline — Key Criterion
✅
Stromal Invasion — the ONLY microscopic feature that definitively distinguishes a malignant surface epithelial tumor from a borderline tumor. All other features (atypia, mitoses, multilayering, papillary growth) may be present in borderline tumors.
📌
Psammoma Bodies — concentrically laminated calcified bodies; characteristic of serous tumors; indicate papillary architecture but NOT malignancy alone.
📌
Signet-Ring Cells — intracytoplasmic mucin pushes nucleus to one side; hallmark of Krukenberg tumor (metastatic) — always search for GIT primary.

🔄 Diagnostic Algorithm: Ovarian Mass
Step 1 — Assess Bilaterality
Bilateral = Theca lutein cyst, PCOS, Krukenberg tumor, or dysgerminoma. Unilateral = most primary tumors.
Step 2 — Check Contents
Dark brown → Chocolate cyst (endometriosis) | Clear → follicular/serous | Mucoid → mucinous | Solid → fibroma/dysgerminoma/immature teratoma
Step 3 — Microscopic Lining
Ciliated columnar → serous | Mucinous → mucinous | Hemosiderin macrophages + glands → endometriotic | Signet ring cells → Krukenberg | Glycogen cells + lymphocytes → dysgerminoma
Step 4 — Look for Stromal Invasion
Present → MALIGNANT carcinoma | Absent (with epithelial complexity) → BORDERLINE | Simple cyst → BENIGN
Step 5 — Clinical Correlation
Age, serum markers (AFP, HCG, CA-125), hormonal symptoms, GIT primary search for bilateral masses

Management

Pathologically-guided management principles for ovarian conditions

🔵
PCOS Management
Lifestyle: Weight loss, exercise → improve insulin resistance → restore ovulation
Metformin: Reduces insulin resistance; first-line for metabolic component
Clomiphene citrate: Ovulation induction for infertility
OCP: Suppresses androgens; regulates cycles; prevents endometrial hyperplasia
Progestin: Opposes estrogen → prevents endometrial hyperplasia progression
Monitor endometrium: Annual ultrasound/biopsy due to unopposed estrogen risk
🔴
Ruptured Corpus Luteum Cyst
Emergency: Acute abdomen + hemoperitoneum → urgent assessment
Hemodynamically stable: Conservative management, observation, analgesia
Hemodynamically unstable: Surgical intervention (laparoscopy/laparotomy), hemostasis
Resuscitation: IV fluids, blood transfusion as needed
🟡
Endometriotic Cyst
Medical: GnRH analogues, danazol, progestins, combined OCP → suppress cyclical shedding
Surgical: Laparoscopic cystectomy; drain and excise wall of chocolate cyst
Fertility: Surgery improves ovarian reserve if planning conception
Malignancy risk: Small risk of clear cell/endometrioid carcinoma arising from endometriosis; surveillance needed
🔵
Surface Epithelial Carcinoma
Surgery: Total abdominal hysterectomy + bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy + omentectomy
Staging: Peritoneal washings, lymph node dissection
Chemotherapy: Platinum-based (carboplatin + paclitaxel) — standard for high-grade serous
BRCA testing: PARP inhibitors (olaparib) for BRCA-mutated high-grade serous carcinoma
Type I (borderline): Conservative fertility-sparing surgery may be possible; excellent prognosis
🟢
Dysgerminoma
Highly radiosensitive + chemosensitive (like seminoma)
Stage I: Unilateral oophorectomy → excellent cure rate; fertility preservation possible
Advanced: BEP chemotherapy (bleomycin, etoposide, cisplatin)
Prognosis: Generally excellent even with metastatic disease due to chemosensitivity
🟣
Meigs Syndrome / Fibroma
Definitive Tx: Surgical removal of ovarian fibroma
Result: Complete spontaneous resolution of ascites AND hydrothorax after tumor excision
No chemotherapy needed — this is a benign condition
Key: Distinguish from malignant ascites — cytology shows NO malignant cells; fibroma histology benign
🔴
Krukenberg Tumor
Find the primary: Upper GI endoscopy to locate gastric primary
Stage IV gastric cancer: Palliative chemotherapy (FOLFOX, capecitabine-based)
Surgical cytoreduction: Selected cases with good performance status
Prognosis: Generally poor — represents advanced/metastatic disease
