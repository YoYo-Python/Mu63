⏱️ Types of Hormonal Contraceptives

💊 Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs)

Contain synthetic estrogen (ethinyl estradiol) + synthetic progestin. Inhibit ovulation via negative feedback on LH & FSH. Taken 21–24 days active + 4–7 days placebo. Available as monophasic, biphasic, or triphasic formulations.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD ⚙️ MECHANISM

💊 Progestogen-Only Pills (POPs / Mini-pills)

Contain only progestin (e.g., norgestrel). Modify cervical mucus, cause endometrial atrophy, variably inhibit ovulation. Taken daily without placebo. Ideal for breastfeeding women (no effect on lactation).

⭐ EXAM FAVORITE 💡 CLINICAL PEARL

💉 Injectable Progestins

Medroxyprogesterone acetate given IM every 3 months. Long-acting. Note: infertility may persist for many months after last injection.

💡 CLINICAL PEARL

📌 Progestin Implants

Subdermally placed (usually upper arm). Contains levonorgestrel. Effective 3–5 years. LARC method (Long-Acting Reversible Contraceptive).

🔄 Progestin Intrauterine System (IUS)

Continuous local low-dose levonorgestrel. Induces endometrial atrophy → dramatically decreases bleeding. Excellent for patients with heavy periods & anemia.

⭐ EXAM FAVORITE

🩹 Transdermal Patch

Delivers ethinyl estradiol + norelgestromin through the skin. Applied to abdomen/upper torso/buttock. Changed weekly for 3 weeks, off for 1 week.

💍 Vaginal Ring

Contains ethinyl estradiol + etonogestrel. Inserted vaginally for 3 weeks, removed for 1 week to allow withdrawal bleeding.

🚨 Emergency Contraception (Postcoital)

Levonorgestrel taken within 72 hours of unprotected intercourse, repeated 12 hours later. NOT a routine method.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD

🎯 Key Clinical Concepts

🔄 Enterohepatic Recycling

Liver conjugates estrogen with glucuronic acid → excreted in bile → intestinal bacteria cleave the bond → free estrogen reabsorbed. Antibiotics kill flora → estrogen excreted in stool → pregnancy.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD ⭐ EXAM FAVORITE

⚡ Enzyme Inducers

Rifampin & Phenytoin induce hepatic CYP enzymes → accelerate estrogen/progestin metabolism → contraceptive failure.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD ⚙️ MECHANISM

🧴 Laxative Interaction

Paraffin oil (liquid paraffin) physically decreases intestinal absorption of oral contraceptives → failure.

⭐ EXAM FAVORITE

⚖️ Weight Gain (#1 Complaint)

Steroid nucleus → salt & water retention → weight gain & edema. Most common patient complaint.

💡 CLINICAL PEARL ⭐ EXAM FAVORITE

❤️ CVS Risk (Smokers >35)

COCs are absolutely contraindicated in smokers >35 years. Risk of MI, thrombosis, hypertension dramatically elevated.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD 💡 CLINICAL PEARL

🍼 Breastfeeding & POPs

Estrogen inhibits lactation → POPs are the "breastfeeding pills." Progestin alone does not affect milk production.

⭐ EXAM FAVORITE 💡 CLINICAL PEARL

🧠 Migraine = STOP COCs

New onset migraine with aura is an absolute contraindication to continuing COCs. Risk of stroke increases 2–10x.

🔥 HIGH-YIELD

🩸 Withdrawal Bleeding

Active pills taken 21–24 days → 4–7 days placebo → hormone levels drop → endometrial shedding = withdrawal bleeding (not true menstruation).

🔄 Enterohepatic Circulation — Step-by-Step

💊 Step 1: Oral Intake

Estrogen (ethinyl estradiol) is absorbed from GI tract into bloodstream

⬇️

🏭 Step 2: Hepatic Conjugation

Liver conjugates estrogen with glucuronic acid → water-soluble conjugate

⬇️

💧 Step 3: Biliary Excretion

Conjugated estrogen excreted via bile into the intestinal lumen

⬇️

🦠 Step 4: Bacterial Cleavage

Intestinal bacteria produce enzymes that cleave the glucuronic acid bond → free active estrogen

⬇️

🔄 Step 5: Reabsorption

Free estrogen is reabsorbed into bloodstream → extends duration of action to ~24 hours

⚠️
Disruption = Contraceptive Failure
3 mechanisms of drug interaction
🦠 Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics
Tetracyclines, Cephalosporins — Kill intestinal flora → no bacterial cleavage → estrogen stays conjugated → excreted in stool
⚡ Enzyme Inducers
Rifampin, Phenytoin — Induce hepatic CYP enzymes → accelerated metabolism → lower hormone levels
🧴 Laxatives
Paraffin oil — Physically decreases intestinal absorption → pills rush through GI tract → inadequate absorption

Ethinyl estradiol + Synthetic progestin

⬇️

Negative feedback → ↓ GnRH → ↓ FSH (estrogen) & ↓ LH (progestin)

No LH surge → no mature follicle release

↑ Cervical mucus thickness + Endometrial changes + Altered tubal motility

🔍 Contraindications & Side Effects

Know when NOT to prescribe & what to watch for

🚫 Absolute Contraindications for COCs

  • ❌Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD)
  • ❌Active or history of thromboembolism (DVT/PE)
  • ❌Smokers over 35 years of age
  • ❌Migraine with aura
  • ❌Breast cancer (estrogen-dependent tumors)
  • ❌Active liver disease / cholestatic hepatitis
  • ❌Pregnancy (known or suspected)
  • ❌Uncontrolled hypertension

⚠️ Side Effects by Organ System

❤️ Cardiovascular (CVS)

  • Hypertension (salt & water retention)
  • Myocardial Infarction (especially smokers >35)
  • Thromboembolism (DVT, PE)
  • Increased thrombotic tendency

🧠 Central Nervous System (CNS)

  • Migraine headache
  • Stroke (cerebral hemorrhage — 2-10x risk)
  • Mood changes & Depression
  • Visual disturbances / Auras

🫄 Gastrointestinal (GIT)

  • Nausea & Vomiting
  • Cholestatic hepatitis
  • Gallstones (cholecystitis)
  • Biliary stasis

🧬 Endocrine / Metabolic

  • Weight gain (#1 complaint — salt/water retention)
  • Edema
  • Hyperglycemia
  • Breast tenderness

💉 Drug Forms & Administration

All hormonal contraceptive options with dosing and schedules

🚨 Emergency / Acute

🆘 Emergency Contraception (Levonorgestrel)
Dose: First dose within 72 hours of unprotected intercourse, second dose 12 hours later.
MOA: High-dose progestin disrupts ovulation/implantation.
Note: NOT a routine contraceptive method. Efficacy decreases with time delay.

💊 Oral Formulations

💊 Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs)
Components: Ethinyl estradiol + synthetic progestin (e.g., norgestrel, desogestrel, drospirenone)
Schedule: 21-24 days active pills + 4-7 days placebo
Types: Monophasic (constant dose), Biphasic (2 dose levels), Triphasic (3 dose levels)
Key: Most reliable oral method when compliant
💊 Progestogen-Only Pills (POPs / Mini-pills)
Components: Progestin only (e.g., norgestrel)
Schedule: Daily without interruption — no placebo week
Ideal for: Breastfeeding women, estrogen contraindications
Downside: Slightly higher failure rate, irregular bleeding

💉 Injectable

💉 Medroxyprogesterone Acetate (Depot)
Route: Intramuscular (IM)
Schedule: Every 3 months
⚠️ Warning: Infertility may persist for many months after final injection
Advantage: No daily compliance needed

📌 Long-Acting Reversible Contraceptives (LARCs)

📌 Subdermal Implant (Levonorgestrel)
Placement: Subdermally in upper arm
Duration: 3-5 years
MOA: Continuous low-dose progestin release
Advantage: Very long-acting, highly effective, reversible
🔄 Progestin IUS (Intrauterine System)
Drug: Levonorgestrel (local release)
Duration: 3-5 years
MOA: Local endometrial atrophy → dramatically ↓ bleeding
Special Use: Excellent for heavy menstrual bleeding & anemia

🩹 Transdermal & Vaginal

🩹 Transdermal Patch
Components: Ethinyl estradiol + Norelgestromin
Application: Abdomen, upper torso, or buttock
Schedule: Change weekly × 3 weeks, off 1 week
Advantage: Weekly rather than daily compliance
💍 Vaginal Ring
Components: Ethinyl estradiol + Etonogestrel
Schedule: Insert for 3 weeks, remove for 1 week
Advantage: Monthly compliance, steady hormone levels
