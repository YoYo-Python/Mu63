📋 Quick Overview

⬆️

Oxytocic Drugs (Uterine Stimulants)

Drugs that stimulate uterine contractions: Oxytocin, Prostaglandins (Dinoprostone, Carboprost, Misoprostol), Mifepristone, Ergot Alkaloids (Methylergometrine). Used for labor induction and PPH.

⬇️

Tocolytic Drugs (Uterine Relaxants)

Drugs that inhibit uterine contractions: Atosiban, β2-agonists (Ritodrine, Salbutamol), CCBs (Nifedipine), MgSO4, PG inhibitors (Indomethacin). Used to prevent preterm labor.

⚠️

Critical Exam Concept

Oxytocics are ONLY for vaginal delivery. Contraindicated in abnormal presentations, C-section, or CPD. Sequence: PGs first (cervical ripening) → then Oxytocin (contractions).

💡

Methylergometrine Rule

NEVER for labor induction — causes tetanic contractions that trap & asphyxiate the fetus. ONLY for postpartum hemorrhage.

🕐 Induction of Labor — Sequential Timeline

Step 1: Cervical Assessment

Evaluate cervix — if closed and unripe, pharmacological ripening is needed before contractions can be induced.

Step 2: Prostaglandins (Dinoprostone / Misoprostol)

Primary mechanism is cervical ripening — softening and dilating the cervix. Given intravaginally or orally. This is the essential first step.

Step 3: Wait Period (Several Hours)

Allow prostaglandins to fully ripen the cervix before starting oxytocin to avoid complications.

Step 4: Oxytocin IV Infusion

Stimulates G-protein coupled receptors → rhythmic uterine contractions from fundus downward → safe expulsion of fetus through the prepared cervix.

Post-Delivery: If Hemorrhage → Methylergometrine

ONLY after baby and placenta are delivered. Causes forceful, prolonged tetanic contraction to compress bleeding vessels.

📋 Diagnostic Algorithm: When to Use Which Oxytocic

Is the patient in labor or post-delivery?
⬇️
PRE-DELIVERY: Is the cervix ripe (soft/dilated)?
⬇️
❌ Cervix NOT ripe → Give Prostaglandins (Dinoprostone) for cervical ripening
⬇️
✅ Cervix ripe → Give Oxytocin IV for rhythmic contractions
⬇️
POST-DELIVERY: Is there uterine hemorrhage?
⬇️
✅ PPH present → Give Methylergometrine IM (tetanic contraction to compress vessels)

💊 Treatment Protocols & Drug Details

🚨
EMERGENCY: Postpartum Hemorrhage — Methylergometrine IM immediately after placenta delivery. If asthmatic, use Oxytocin instead. NEVER Carboprost in asthma.
⚠️
MgSO₄ Toxicity Protocol: If patient develops hypotension + severe muscle weakness → administer IV Calcium Gluconate slowly. Check patellar reflexes regularly.

💉 Oxytocic Drugs — Detailed Cards

💉 Oxytocin
Peptide Hormone | IV/IM
MOA: Gq-coupled receptor → PLC → IP3 → ↑Ca²⁺ → rhythmic contractions
Uses: Labor induction, augmentation, PPH control, milk ejection
Side Effects: Water intoxication (ADH-like), uterine hyperstimulation
CI: Abnormal presentation, CPD, prior uterine surgery
🧪 Dinoprostone (PGE2)
Prostaglandin | Intravaginal
MOA: Stimulates PG receptors → cervical ripening (primary) + uterine contraction
Uses: Cervical ripening before oxytocin, medical abortion
Side Effects: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, fever
⚡ Carboprost (PGF2α)
Prostaglandin | IM
MOA: Stimulates FP receptors → strong uterine contraction + bronchoconstriction
Uses: Refractory PPH, abortion
CI: ⚠️ ASTHMA — causes severe bronchospasm
💊 Misoprostol (PGE1)
Prostaglandin | Oral/Sublingual/Vaginal
MOA: Stimulates EP receptors → cervical ripening + uterine contraction
Uses: Cervical ripening, medical abortion (with Mifepristone), PPH prevention
🔴 Methylergometrine
Ergot Alkaloid | IM
MOA: α-adrenergic + serotonin → forceful TETANIC contractions
Uses: Postpartum hemorrhage ONLY
Side Effects: Hypertension, coronary vasospasm → MI
CI: ⚠️ NEVER during labor, hypertension, coronary artery disease
🚫 Mifepristone (RU-486)
Progesterone Antagonist | Oral
MOA: Competitive progesterone receptor antagonist → reverses uterine relaxation
Uses: Medical termination of pregnancy (with misoprostol)
CI: Ectopic pregnancy, adrenal insufficiency

🛑 Tocolytic Drugs — Detailed Cards

🔄 Atosiban
Oxytocin Receptor Antagonist | IV
MOA: Modified oxytocin → competitive antagonist at OT receptors → stops contractions
Uses: Preterm labor (24–33 weeks)
Side Effects: Hot flushing, nausea, headache
💨 Ritodrine / Salbutamol
β2-Sympathomimetic | IV/Oral
MOA: β2-receptor → ↑cAMP → ↓Ca²⁺ → uterine relaxation
Side Effects: ⚠️ Hypokalemia (K⁺ shift into cells), tachycardia, tremors
CI: Cardiac disease, diabetes
💛 Nifedipine
Calcium Channel Blocker | Oral
MOA: Blocks L-type Ca²⁺ channels → ↓intracellular Ca²⁺ → relaxation
Uses: Preterm labor, hypertension
Side Effects: Hypotension, headache, flushing
🧲 Magnesium Sulphate
Electrolyte | IV
MOA: Ca²⁺ efflux from cells + blocks Ca²⁺ entry → uterine relaxation
Uses: Tocolysis, seizure prevention in preeclampsia/eclampsia
Side Effects: Muscle weakness, hypotension, respiratory depression
CI: ⚠️ MYASTHENIA GRAVIS | Antidote: IV CALCIUM
🔥 Indomethacin
NSAID / PG Inhibitor | Oral/Rectal
MOA: Inhibits COX → ↓PG synthesis → ↓uterine contractions
Uses: Dysmenorrhea, preterm labor (early)
CI: ⚠️ Late 2nd / 3rd trimester → premature closure of ductus arteriosus
