Topic Overview

Male Reproductive System & Pelvic Floor — High-Yield Concepts

Spermatogenesis Timeline

WEEK 7–9
Y chromosome activates Sertoli cells → secrete H-Y antigen → induces testicular differentiation. Leydig cells appear and begin secreting testosterone under HCG influence.
FETAL MONTHS 7–9
Testosterone-driven descent of testes from abdomen to scrotum. Premature birth before this window → cryptorchidism.
PUBERTY
Hypothalamus → GnRH → Anterior pituitary → LH targets Leydig cells, FSH targets Sertoli cells. Spermatogenesis begins in earnest. Testosterone causes epiphyseal closure and secondary sex characteristics.
ADULT
~120 million sperm/day produced. Spermatogonia → primary spermatocyte → secondary spermatocyte → spermatids → spermatozoa (spermiogenesis). Sperm matured in epididymis, stored in vas deferens.

Key Concept Cards

Sertoli Cells

Large pyramidal cells on basal lamina of seminiferous tubules. Form tight junctions creating the blood-testis barrier. Provide nutrition, mechanical support, and remove excess cytoplasm during spermiogenesis. Secrete inhibin to suppress FSH via negative feedback.

FSH-regulated ⚠️ Blood-Testis Barrier H-Y Antigen Source
Leydig Cells

Interstitial cells between seminiferous tubules. Secrete testosterone. Stimulated by HCG in fetal life, LH at puberty. Essential for secondary sex organs, descent of testes, and driving spermatogenesis via androgen support of spermatogonia division.

⚠️ LH Target Testosterone Source HCG → LH switch
Levator Ani Muscle

Major pelvic diaphragm. Three fiber groups: anterior (pubococcygeus) — elevates prostate/vagina, forms sphincter vaginae; intermediate (puborectalis) — sling at anorectal junction; posterior (iliococcygeus) — attaches to coccyx. Injury → uterine prolapse.

⚠️ Always in Exam Episiotomy Risk 4th Sacral Nerve
Internal Iliac Artery Divisions

Posterior division: Superior gluteal, iliolumbar, lateral sacral ("صالح لازم يصلي"). Anterior division: Obturator, internal pudendal, inferior gluteal, middle rectal, superior vesical, inferior vesical (♂) / vaginal (♀), uterine. Middle rectal is a favorite MCQ distractor!

⚠️ Always in Exam ⭕ Middle Rectal Anterior vs Posterior
Temperature Regulation of Testes

Spermatogenesis requires 33–35°C (2–4°C below core). Maintained by: scrotal location away from body heat, thin fat-free scrotal skin for radiation, pampiniform plexus (countercurrent heat exchange with testicular artery), and dartos muscle (contracts in cold, relaxes in heat).

33–35°C Optimal Pampiniform Plexus
Cryptorchidism

Undescended testes. Most common cause: premature birth (testes descend in final 2–3 months of gestation under testosterone drive). Management: HCG hormonal therapy to stimulate testosterone, then surgical orchidopexy. Risk if untreated: infertility, malignancy.

⚠️ Most Common Cause = Prematurity Infertility Risk
Sacral Plexus Formation

Formed by ventral rami of L4, L5, S1, S2, S3, S4. L4+L5 = lumbosacral trunk. Dorsal surface branches: superior gluteal, inferior gluteal, nerve to piriformis. Ventral surface branches: nerve to obturator internus, quadratus femoris. Terminal: sciatic, pudendal nerves.

L4–S4 Lumbosacral Trunk
Spermiogenesis

Transformation of haploid spermatids into spermatozoa. Sertoli cells remove excess cytoplasm. Key changes: acrosome cap forms (contains hydrolytic enzymes for zona pellucida penetration), flagellum develops, mitochondria spiral around midpiece, nucleus condenses. Motility acquired in epididymis.

Sertoli removes cytoplasm Haploid stage
Ganglion Impar

The two sympathetic trunks descend medial to anterior sacral foramina and unite inferiorly to form the unpaired ganglion impar (also called ganglion of Walther). This is the terminal ganglion of the sympathetic chain. Common exam question in pelvis neuroscience.

Exam Favourite Terminal Sympathetic
Sperm Storage & Maturation

Sperm are produced in seminiferous tubules, travel through rete testis → epididymis (primary maturation site, ~12 days) → vas deferens (primary storage site). Sperm acquire forward motility during epididymal transit. Vasectomy interrupts vas deferens.

Epididymis = Maturation Vas Deferens = Storage

LEYDIG CELLS TIMELINE

Appear and secrete testosterone at 7–9 weeks. HCG controls in fetus, LH at puberty.

VITAMIN C → TESTOSTERONE

Vitamin C specifically depresses testosterone synthesis. Vit A → keratinization. Vit E → tubular degeneration.

Diagnostic Algorithm — Male Infertility

Step 1: Semen analysis → Count, motility, morphology
Step 2: Hormone panel — FSH, LH, Testosterone, Prolactin
Step 3: If LH/FSH high + low T → Primary hypogonadism (testicular cause) → Testicular biopsy / ultrasound
Step 4: If LH/FSH low + low T → Secondary hypogonadism (pituitary/hypothalamic) → MRI pituitary
Step 5: Exclude extrinsic factors — fever, radiation, toxins, nutritional deficits (Vit A, C, E, B12)
Step 6: Temperature-related? → Check for varicocele, cryptorchidism, occupation (hot environments)

Management & Treatment

Reproductive & pelvic disorder management

🔴 CRYPTORCHIDISM — URGENT
1st: HCG hormonal therapy (stimulates Leydig cells → testosterone → encourage descent)
2nd: Orchidopexy (surgical fixation) if unresponsive
Timing: Before age 2 to preserve fertility
Untreated risk: Infertility, testicular malignancy
🔴 SECONDARY HYPOGONADISM
Exogenous testosterone replacement therapy
Or: Gonadotropin therapy (HCG + FSH) if fertility desired
Root cause: Identify pituitary/hypothalamic lesion (MRI)
No inherent compensatory mechanism exists
🟡 UTERINE PROLAPSE (Levator Ani Injury)
Conservative: Pelvic floor exercises (Kegel)
Pessary ring for temporary support
Surgical: Anterior/posterior colporrhaphy
Prevention: Proper episiotomy repair; avoid over-widening
🟡 TEMPERATURE-RELATED INFERTILITY
Remove extrinsic heat sources (tight clothing, hot baths)
Varicocele: Embolization or ligation
Cryptorchidism: See above
Recovery: Spermatogenesis recovers in ~3 months
🟢 NUTRITIONAL DEFICIENCY
Vitamin A supplementation → reverses germinal keratinization
Vitamin C supplementation → restores testosterone synthesis
Vitamin E → antioxidant protection of tubules
Balanced diet; correct starvation state
🟢 MUMPS ORCHITIS
Supportive care (bed rest, scrotal support, NSAIDs)
Corticosteroids in severe cases
Prevention: MMR vaccine (most effective approach)
Prognosis: If bilateral, risk of permanent infertility
