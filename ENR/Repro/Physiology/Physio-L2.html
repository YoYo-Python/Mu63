🔑 Core Concepts

Follicle Development

Primordial → Primary (granulosa proliferate) → Secondary/Antral (theca cells + antrum) → Mature Graafian follicle. 400,000 at puberty.

Preovulatory Phase

Estrogen Synthesis

Theca interna → LH → Androgens → Granulosa cells → Aromatase (FSH-driven) → Estrogen. Two-cell, two-gonadotropin model.

⚠️ High Yield

LH Surge & Ovulation

Massive estrogen rise → positive feedback on anterior pituitary → LH surge 2 days before ovulation → release of haploid secondary oocyte.

⭐ Most Famous +ve Feedback

Corpus Luteum

Remnant granulosa + theca cells → secretes progesterone + estrogen. Maintained to week 12 of pregnancy by hCG. Degenerates in 10 days if no fertilization.

hCG Maintains CL

Secretory Phase

Days 14–28, always constant 14 days. Progesterone-driven. Glands tortuous + store glycogen/lipids. Prepares endometrium for implantation.

Always 14 Days!

BBT & Progesterone

Progesterone from corpus luteum → thermogenic effect on hypothalamus → 0.5°C rise in BBT post-ovulation, persisting until day 26.

⚠️ Exam Favorite

Cervical Mucus

Estrogen → thin, watery, ferning + spinnbarkeit. Progesterone → thick, viscid, cellular. Abolishes ferning pattern in luteal phase.

Clinical Sign

Myometrial Quieting

Progesterone → anti-estrogenic effect on myometrium → decreases excitability + oxytocin sensitivity. Critical for maintaining pregnancy.

Pregnancy Protection

Progesterone: Natriuresis

Large doses block aldosterone action on the kidney → natriuresis (sodium excretion). Unique metabolic effect of progesterone.

MCQ Trap!
⚠️
EXAM KEY: The most famous example of positive feedback in the human body is the effect of high estrogen on LH. The secretory phase is always constant at 14 days — variations in cycle length come from changes in the proliferative phase duration.
🏆
HIGH YIELD: Females are born with a limited, fixed number of follicles (oogenesis is complete before birth). Males maintain continuous spermatogenesis throughout life. This explains menopause — when follicles are exhausted.

⚗️ Physiology & Hormones

Interactive hormone action comparison, two-cell model, and feedback mechanisms.

🔄 Feedback Loop: HPO Axis

🧠 Hypothalamus
Secretes GnRH (Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone) in a pulsatile manner. Receives feedback from estrogen and progesterone.
↓ GnRH
🫘 Anterior Pituitary
Secretes FSH (follicle stimulating hormone) and LH (luteinizing hormone) in response to GnRH.
↓ FSH + LH
🌸 Ovarian Follicle
FSH → granulosa cell proliferation + aromatase activation. LH → theca interna androgen synthesis.
↑ Estrogen rises gradually
⚡ Estrogen Peak (>200 pg/mL for 48h)
Critical threshold: when estrogen rises sharply and is sustained, it switches from NEGATIVE to POSITIVE feedback on the pituitary!
↑ Positive feedback!
🚀 LH SURGE (most famous +ve feedback in humans!)
The LH surge occurs 2 days before ovulation. This is the most famous example of positive feedback in human physiology.
↓ 36–40 hours later
💥 OVULATION — Release of Secondary Oocyte
The secondary oocyte (haploid, 23 chromosomes) is released into the peritoneal cavity. Occurs on day ~14 of a 28-day cycle.

💊 Hormone Actions Comparison

💗 Estrogen Actions
🌸 Stimulates follicle growth and development
⚡ Generates LH surge via positive feedback on anterior pituitary
🧫 Induces endometrial cellular proliferation (proliferative phase)
💧 Makes cervical mucus thin + watery → ferning + spinnbarkeit
🦷 Converts vaginal epithelium: cuboidal → stratified squamous
🦴 Protein-anabolic effect on bone; promotes growth, closes epiphyseal plates
🫀 Increases plasma proteins (TBG, SHBG, ceruloplasmin)
🧪 Increases vaginal glycogen → lactic acid → low pH (acidic, protective)
🌿 Progesterone Actions
🌀 Induces secretory changes in endometrium (tortuous glands, glycogen)
🤫 Decreases myometrial excitability (anti-estrogenic = maintains pregnancy)
🌡️ Thermogenic — raises BBT by 0.5°C on hypothalamus
🔒 Makes cervical mucus thick, viscid, cellular → blocks sperm
🧂 Large doses → natriuresis (blocks aldosterone)
🚫 Negative feedback on hypothalamus + pituitary → inhibits LH/FSH
🫁 Stimulates respiratory center → increased alveolar ventilation
🩺 Large doses → abolishes LH surge (mechanism of progesterone-only OCP)

📅 Cycle & Ovulation

Detailed breakdown of the uterine cycle, follicular development stages, and clinical signs of ovulation.

🗓️ Uterine (Menstrual) Cycle

🩸 Menstrual Phase

Days 1–5. Corpus luteum degenerates → ↓ E₂ + P₄ → withdrawal of hormonal support → shedding of functional endometrial layer (stratum functionale).

🌱 Proliferative Phase

Days 5–14. Estrogen-driven (FSH → follicle growth → E₂). Endometrium regenerates from basal layer. Variable duration — accounts for cycle length variation!

🌿 Secretory Phase

Days 14–28. Always constant 14 days. Progesterone from corpus luteum → tortuous glands, glycogen + lipid storage. Prepares for implantation. Pregnanediol in urine confirms this phase.

🌸 Follicular Development Stages

🥚
Primordial Follicle
400,000 at puberty. Primary oocyte arrested in prophase I. Single layer flat granulosa cells. Fixed pool — no regeneration!
→
🌱
Primary Follicle
Granulosa cells proliferate (cuboidal). Zona pellucida forms around oocyte. No antrum yet.
→
💧
Secondary/Antral Follicle
Theca interna + externa differentiate. Antrum forms (follicular fluid rich in estrogen secreted by granulosa cells). FSH + LH both active.
→
🌕
Mature Graafian Follicle
Large antrum. Oocyte on cumulus oophorus. Ready for LH surge → ovulation. Highest estrogen output.

💥 Ovulation — Clinical Signs

🌡️
BBT Rise (+0.5°C)

Progesterone thermogenic effect on hypothalamus after corpus luteum forms. Persists days 15–26.

🔬
Ferning Disappears

Pre-ovulation: ferning present (estrogen). Post-ovulation: ferning absent (progesterone thickens mucus).

💊
Pregnanediol in Urine

Urinary metabolite of progesterone. Presence confirms active corpus luteum and ovulation occurred.

🏥
Endometrial Biopsy

Secretory changes (tortuous glands, glycogen) on biopsy from 2nd half of cycle confirm ovulation.

📉
LH Surge Detection

Urine/serum LH surge detectable 24–36h before ovulation. Basis of ovulation predictor kits.

😣
Mittelschmerz

Mid-cycle pelvic pain at time of ovulation. Due to follicular rupture and peritoneal irritation by follicular fluid.

🔗 Integration — Long Case

A step-by-step analysis of the integrative anovulatory cycle scenario from your notes.

📋 Clinical Presentation

A 26-year-old female presents to a fertility clinic. Her charts show no basal body temperature rise throughout her 28-day cycle. Cervical mucus remains thin and shows continuous ferning and spinnbarkeit phenomena up to day 26. She is trying to conceive.

1
Identify the Process

Anovulatory cycle — failure to ovulate. Without the LH surge, the Graafian follicle does not rupture. No corpus luteum forms. The ovary continues to secrete estrogen (from follicle) but no progesterone is produced.

2
Step-by-Step Mechanism

Normal: High E₂ → LH surge → ovulation → corpus luteum → P₄ secretion → BBT rise + cervical mucus thickening (ferning disappears).

In this patient: Ovulation failed → no corpus luteum → no P₄ → no thermogenic effect → no change in cervical mucus → continuous ferning reflects unopposed estrogen.

3
Alternative Explanations

① Pituitary failure to generate LH surge (e.g., hypopituitarism).
② Insufficient estrogen to trigger positive feedback (follicle fails to mature sufficiently).
③ LH receptor deficiency on granulosa/theca cells.

4
Best Investigations

① Urinary pregnanediol — absent → confirms no progesterone production → confirms anovulation.
② Endometrial biopsy (day 21–22) — proliferative pattern (not secretory) → confirms anovulation.
③ Serum P₄ level on day 21 — if <3 ng/mL → anovulatory.

5
Consequences of Disruption

Without ovulation, the endometrium remains in a prolonged proliferative state driven by unopposed estrogen. This causes:
• Infertility (no egg released)
• Irregular, unpredictable uterine bleeding
• Long-term: endometrial hyperplasia risk (unopposed estrogen)

6
Broader Principle

Progesterone is exclusively produced by the corpus luteum in the second half of the cycle. Its absence in the luteal phase (days 14–28) is the definitive biochemical marker of anovulation.

7
Compensatory Mechanisms

The overgrown endometrium eventually outgrows its blood supply → ischemia → unpredictable sloughing (dysfunctional uterine bleeding / DUB). This is NOT organized like normal menstruation — it lacks the progesterone withdrawal trigger, making it irregular and prolonged.
