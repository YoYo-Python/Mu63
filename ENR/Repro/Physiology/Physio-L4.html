🤰 Mechanism of Labor

High-Yield

Increased E/P ratio → ↑ myometrial excitability + ↑ oxytocin receptors. Uterine distension evokes spinal reflexes. Positive feedback loop drives delivery.

🔄 Positive Feedback Loop

High-YieldClinical Pearl

Fetal push → cervical stretch → hypothalamus → posterior pituitary → oxytocin → stronger contractions → more stretch → repeat until delivery.

🩸 Postpartum Hemostasis

Clinical Pearl

Contracting uterine muscle fibers mechanically compress vessels. Prostaglandins (PGs) cause vasoconstriction. Failure = postpartum hemorrhage.

🍼 Mammary Development

High-Yield

Estrogens → fat deposition + duct growth. Progesterone (needs E priming) → secretory alveoli. Permissive factors: insulin, GH, glucocorticoids, prolactin.

🥛 Initiation of Lactation

High-YieldExam Fav

Placental delivery → sharp ↓ estrogen → removes blockade of prolactin receptors → prolactin binds → colostrum (days 1-3) then milk.

👶 Suckling Reflex

High-Yield

Nipple touch → spinal cord → hypothalamus → ① inhibits PIH (↑ prolactin for secretion) ② stimulates PVN → oxytocin (↑ myoepithelial contraction for ejection).

🚫 Lactational Amenorrhea

Clinical Pearl

High prolactin → inhibits GnRH → ↓ FSH/LH → no ovulation. Lasts 6-7 months of continuous nursing, but unreliable after ~3 months as prolactin drops.

💊 OCP Mechanisms

High-Yield

Estrogen → inhibits FSH (no follicle growth). Progesterone → inhibits LH surge (no ovulation) + thickens cervical mucus + alters endometrium.

🌡️ Menopause

Exam Fav

Depletion of 300k-400k primordial follicles (age 45-55) → ↓ estrogen → vasomotor instability (hot flushes), osteoporosis, secondary sex organ atrophy (clitoris spared).

⚗️ Pituitary Stalk Lesson

Clinical Pearl

Cutting the stalk → prolactin RISES (not falls). Reason: dominant hypothalamic signal is inhibitory (PIH/dopamine). PRH is weaker than PIH.

🦴 Relaxin in Labor

Supporting

Relaxin softens the pubic symphysis and cervix, facilitating delivery. Distinct from oxytocin's role in contractions.

💉 Drug-Induced Galactorrhea

Clinical Pearl

Dopamine blockers (e.g., chlorpromazine, metoclopramide) → remove PIH effect → ↑ prolactin → galactorrhea (milk in non-pregnant state).

Labor Timeline

Sequence of events from late pregnancy to postpartum

📅 Last Month of Pregnancy

↑ Estrogen/Progesterone ratio → ↑ oxytocin receptors in myometrium. Uterine distension increases mechanoreceptors and spinal reflexes are evoked.

🔄 Onset of Labor

Baby descends → cervix stretches → afferent impulses to hypothalamus → posterior pituitary releases oxytocin → contractions intensify.

👶 Delivery

Positive feedback culminates in expulsion of the baby. The positive feedback loop is terminated (no more stretch stimulus).

🩹 Placental Expulsion

Continued uterine contractions expel the placenta (afterbirth).

🛑 Hemostasis

Contracting muscle fibers compress uterine vessels. PGs cause vasoconstriction. Bleeding stops.

📉 Estrogen Drop

Loss of placenta → sharp ↓ in estrogen → prolactin receptor blockade removed → milk secretion initiated.

🥛 Colostrum → Milk

Days 1-3: colostrum (protein-rich). Then: true milk (proteins + lactose + fats). Maintained by suckling reflex.

Dual Roles of Estrogen ⚠️ High-Yield

Understanding estrogen's paradox is the key to mastering this topic

Estrogen as ACTIVATOR

  • ↑ Myometrial excitability (labor)
  • ↑ Oxytocin receptors (labor)
  • ↑ Ductal growth in breast
  • ↑ Fat deposition in breast
  • Primes breast for progesterone action

Estrogen as INHIBITOR

  • Blocks prolactin receptors in breast (prevents lactation during pregnancy)
  • Inhibits FSH (via OCPs)
  • High levels suppress lactogenesis until drop occurs

Clinical Management

Treatment strategies based on the physiology you've learned

🚨 Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)

  • Oxytocin infusion → uterotonic
  • Ergometrine → sustained uterine contraction
  • Misoprostol (PGE1) → uterotonic + vasoconstriction
  • Uterine massage to compress vessels
  • Surgical: B-Lynch suture, hysterectomy if refractory

⚠️ Augmentation/Induction of Labor

  • IV Oxytocin (Syntocinon) — titrated carefully
  • Cervical ripening: PGE2 (dinoprostone)
  • Amniotomy (AROM)
  • Monitor: fetal heart rate! Excessive oxytocin → fetal distress

🥛 Lactation Support

  • Encourage frequent suckling (maintains prolactin)
  • Metoclopramide (dopamine blocker) → ↑ prolactin if supply low
  • Cabergoline/Bromocriptine → dopamine agonist to STOP lactation
  • Engorgement: regular feeding, warm compress

🌡️ Menopause Management (HRT)

  • Combined HRT (E+P): reduces hot flushes, protects bone
  • Estrogen-only HRT: post-hysterectomy
  • SERMs (Raloxifene): bone protection without uterine risk
  • Non-hormonal: SSRIs for hot flushes, bisphosphonates for osteoporosis
  • Calcium + Vitamin D supplementation

💊 Contraception After Delivery

  • Lactational amenorrhea: unreliable after 3 months
  • Progestogen-only pill: safe in breastfeeding
  • Combined OCP: avoid until 6 weeks postpartum (DVT risk)
  • IUCD: highly effective, non-hormonal option
  • Barrier methods: always backup for amenorrhea reliance

⚗️ Galactorrhea (Non-Puerperal)

  • Stop offending drug (metoclopramide, antipsychotics)
  • Dopamine agonists (Cabergoline, Bromocriptine) → ↑ PIH → ↓ prolactin
  • If prolactinoma: medical first (cabergoline), then surgery
  • Check: thyroid (hypothyroidism ↑ TRH → ↑ prolactin)
