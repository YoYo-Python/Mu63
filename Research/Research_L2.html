Core Concepts

Definitions, design logic, and the indications that make case-control the right tool for the job.

  • Case-control is an observational, analytical epidemiology study design.
  • It tests etiologic hypotheses by evaluating links between exposure and disease outcomes.
  • The design is strictly retrospective — it looks backward from an existing outcome to determine past exposure.
  • CASESubjects properly diagnosed with the disease or outcome of interest, based on strict inclusion criteria.
  • CONTROLHealthy subjects entirely free from the studied disease, drawn from the same population.
  • Matching: selecting controls that share specific confounding characteristics (e.g., age, sex) with cases to isolate the true exposure risk.
  • INDICATIONMost optimal design for investigating rare diseases.
  • INDICATIONHighly effective for diseases with exceptionally long latency periods.
  • INDICATIONIdeal when needing to simultaneously investigate multiple risk factors for a single disease outcome.

  Formulas

  • P1 (exposed among cases) = a / (a + c)
  • P2 (exposed among controls) = b / (b + d)
  • Relative Contribution = P1 − P2
  • Odds Ratio (OR) = (a × d) / (b × c)

Scenario 1 · High-Carbohydrate Diet and Dental Caries

36 caries cases (32 exposed, 4 not) · 64 healthy controls (16 exposed, 48 not).

Show Steps
  1. Identify 2×2 values: a=32, b=16, c=4, d=48
  2. Apply formula: OR = (32 × 48) / (16 × 4)
  3. Calculate: OR = 1536 / 64 = 24
  4. Interpretation: carbohydrate exposure accompanies a 24-times increased risk of caries.

Scenario 2 · Smoking and Lung Cancer

100 cancer cases (50 smokers, 50 non-smokers) · 100 healthy controls (15 smokers, 85 non-smokers).

Show Steps
  1. Identify 2×2 values: a=50, b=15, c=50, d=85
  2. Apply formula: OR = (50 × 85) / (15 × 50)
  3. Calculate: OR = 4250 / 750 ≈ 5.67
  4. Interpretation: smokers are 5.67× more likely to develop lung cancer.

Scenario 3 · Exercise and Obesity (OR < 1)

100 obese cases (20 exercised, 80 did not) · 100 healthy controls (70 exercised, 30 did not).

Show Steps
  1. Identify 2×2 values: a=20, b=70, c=80, d=30
  2. Apply formula: OR = (20 × 30) / (70 × 80)
  3. Calculate: OR = 600 / 5600 ≈ 0.10
  4. Interpretation: OR < 1 confirms exercise is a protective factor against obesity.
04

Exam Traps

Eight ways examiners try to catch you out on this topic.

1 · Rare disease ≠ rare exposure

Case-control is for rare diseases; it is completely ineffective for rare exposures.

2 · No incidence, no prevalence

Case-control studies cannot calculate true disease rates.

3 · OR = 1 misread

OR = 1 means absolute zero association between exposure and disease, not "low risk."

4 · OR < 1 is not an error

It strictly indicates a protective factor — exposure decreases disease.

5 · Forgetting to match

Without matching controls to cases on confounders (like age), results are statistically invalid.

6 · Bias ignorance

Retrospective design relies on memory/records — heavily liable to recall and selection bias.

7 · Direct risk calculation

Case-control calculates risk indirectly (Odds Ratio), not directly (Relative Risk).

8 · Flawed sample ratios

An unbalanced case-to-control ratio (e.g., 100 cases but only 20 controls) invalidates the study.
