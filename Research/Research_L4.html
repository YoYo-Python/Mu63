Experimental Studies

RCT design, trial phases, bias control, and efficacy calculations

Core Concepts

Experimental Study
A prospective study intentionally introducing a treatment/intervention to observe outcomes and confirm etiological hypotheses.
Parallel Trial
An experimental design where each group concurrently receives only one assigned treatment. No crossover occurs.
Crossover Trial
A design where groups receive all treatments sequentially, requiring a washout period between treatments to eliminate carryover effects.
Factorial Trial
A design allocating two or more treatments per group to test both combined and separate effects simultaneously.
Quasi-experimental Study
A non-randomized trial assessing outcomes via pre-test and post-test measurements. Lacks the gold-standard random allocation of an RCT.
Randomization
Unbiased chance allocation of subjects to control confounding variables and eliminate selection bias. Not the same as blinding.
Blinding (Masking)
Hiding group allocation (using placebos) from subjects and/or investigators to prevent subjective observation bias. Not the same as randomization.
Phase I Trial
Tests drug safety, tolerance, and pharmacokinetics on 20–100 healthy volunteers. Exception: severely ill patients (e.g., cancer) when the drug has serious side effects.
Phase III Trial
Large randomized controlled trial (500–1500 subjects) assessing efficacy against competing drugs for market approval. The definitive evidence phase.

Formulas & Calculations

Absolute Risk Reduction
ARR = | EER − CER |
EER = Experimental Event Rate · CER = Control Event Rate
Relative Risk Reduction
RRR = | EER − CER | / CER
Always divide by CER — never by EER (common trap!)
Number Needed to Treat
NNT = 1 / ARR
Always round up. Smaller NNT = more effective drug.

Worked Clinical Scenarios

Scenario 1 — Antihypertensive Drug (Stroke)
CER (stroke in control) = 0.40 · EER (stroke on drug) = 0.10
Show Steps
1
ARR = |0.10 − 0.40| = 0.30
2
RRR = 0.30 / 0.40 = 0.75 (75% efficacy)
3
NNT = 1 / 0.30 = 3.33 → Round up to 4 patients
